## Break-Even Analysis Methodology

Every service business owner must know their break-even point — the revenue level where total costs equal total revenue and profit is zero.

### Break-even formula
**Break-even revenue = Fixed Costs / Gross Margin Percentage**

### Example: 8-person painting company
- Monthly fixed costs: $28,000 (rent $2,500 + insurance $1,200 + vehicle payments $3,800 + admin salary $4,500 + software $600 + loan payments $2,400 + utilities $800 + licenses $200 + misc $2,000 + owner draw $10,000)
- Gross margin: 45% (after labor, materials, subcontractors)
- Break-even: $28,000 / 0.45 = **$62,222/month**
- That means roughly $747,000/year before the business earns one dollar of profit

### When to recalculate
- Any time fixed costs change by more than 5%
- After adding or losing employees
- When material costs shift significantly
- Quarterly at minimum, monthly during growth phases

## NPV and Payback Period for Equipment/Expansion

### Payback period (simple version)
**Payback period = Total Investment / Annual Net Cash Flow from Investment**

A plumber considering a $45,000 jetter machine:
- Additional revenue enabled: $8,000/month
- Additional costs (maintenance, insurance, fuel): $1,500/month
- Net monthly cash flow: $6,500
- Payback period: $45,000 / $6,500 = **6.9 months**

**Rule of thumb:** For equipment under $100K, target payback under 18 months. For equipment over $100K, target payback under 36 months.

### Net Present Value (simplified for small business)
For larger decisions ($50K+), account for the time value of money. Use a discount rate of 10-15% for small service businesses (reflects risk and opportunity cost).

**NPV decision rule:** If NPV is positive, the investment creates value. Compare competing investments by NPV, not just payback period.

### Discount rate selection (2025-2026)
- Low risk expansion (proven service, existing market): 10%
- Medium risk (new service line, adjacent market): 12-15%
- High risk (new geography, unproven concept): 18-20%

## Hiring vs Outsourcing Decision Matrix

| Factor | Hire | Outsource |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Work volume | Consistent 30+ hrs/week | Sporadic or seasonal |
| Skill availability | Trainable within 2 weeks | Specialized/rare skill |
| Quality control | Critical to brand | Acceptable at contractor standard |
| Duration of need | 12+ months | Under 6 months |
| Cost at volume | Cheaper at scale | Cheaper at low volume |
| Liability | You carry workers comp + liability | Contractor carries own insurance |
| Typical break-even | 20-25 billable hours/week | Below 20 hours/week |

### Cost comparison framework (2025-2026)
- **W-2 employee fully loaded cost:** 1.25-1.4x base wage (includes FICA 7.65%, workers comp 1-5%, benefits, PTO)
- **1099 subcontractor rate:** Typically 1.3-1.6x what you would pay a W-2 employee hourly, but zero benefits cost and no downtime pay
- **Break-even calculation:** If you need 25+ hours/week of the same skill for 6+ months, hiring is almost always cheaper

### IRS classification risk
Misclassifying W-2 workers as 1099 contractors carries penalties of $50 per W-2 not filed plus 1.5% of wages plus 40% of FICA not withheld. In 2025, IRS increased enforcement audits on construction and trades by 22%. Use the IRS 20-factor test or consult an accountant.

## Build vs Buy Evaluation

Applies to systems, processes, and capabilities — not just software.

### Build when
- The capability is a core differentiator (your estimating methodology, your training program)
- No off-the-shelf solution fits your workflow
- You have internal expertise to maintain it
- Long-term cost of ownership is lower than licensing

### Buy when
- The capability is commodity (accounting, payroll, CRM)
- Speed to implementation matters more than customization
- The vendor invests more in R&D than you ever could
- Maintenance and updates are included

### Common decisions for service businesses
- **Accounting:** Always buy (QuickBooks, Xero) — $30-200/month
- **Estimating:** Buy a tool, customize templates — $30-150/month
- **Training program:** Build internally, unique to your standards
- **Dispatch/scheduling:** Buy — $50-200/month (Jobber, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro)
- **Website:** Buy template + customize — $2,000-8,000 one-time, $50-200/month hosting
- **Safety program:** Build with industry templates as starting point

## Scenario Planning: Best/Base/Worst Case

Every major decision (hiring, expansion, equipment purchase, new service line) needs three scenarios.

### Structure
| Scenario | Revenue assumption | Cost assumption | Probability |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Best case** | 120% of target | On budget | 20% |
| **Base case** | 100% of target | 105% of budget | 50% |
| **Worst case** | 70% of target | 115% of budget | 30% |

### Worst-case survival test
The decision passes only if the business survives the worst case for 6 months without emergency measures. If worst case requires layoffs, emergency loans, or owner not taking a draw for 3+ months, the decision is too risky at current scale.

### Seasonal adjustment
Service businesses must factor seasonality. A landscaper's worst case in January is different from July. Run scenarios against the weakest quarter, not the annual average.

## Capital Allocation Framework

### Four-stage process
1. **Idea:** Identify the opportunity or need (new truck, additional crew, marketing campaign)
2. **Analysis:** Calculate ROI, payback period, and run scenario planning
3. **Planning:** Budget the capital, identify funding source, set timeline and milestones
4. **Monitoring:** Track actual vs projected performance monthly for 6 months post-investment

### Allocation priorities (2025-2026 small service business)
1. **Cash reserves:** 60-90 days operating expenses before any discretionary investment
2. **Revenue-generating assets:** Equipment and people that directly produce billable work
3. **Efficiency investments:** Software and systems that reduce waste (typically 10-25% ROI)
4. **Growth investments:** Marketing, new markets, new services (higher risk, higher potential return)
5. **Comfort/quality-of-life:** Office upgrades, non-essential vehicles — last priority

### Funding source hierarchy
1. **Retained earnings** (cheapest, no interest, no obligation)
2. **Business line of credit** (flexible, 7-12% interest in 2025-2026)
3. **Equipment financing** (asset-backed, 6-12% interest, preserves cash)
4. **SBA loan** (lowest rates 6-9%, but slow approval 30-90 days)
5. **Credit cards** (emergency only, 18-28% APR destroys margins)

### Decision speed by dollar amount
- Under $5,000: Owner decides same day with basic math
- $5,000-25,000: One-week analysis with payback calculation
- $25,000-100,000: Full scenario planning, 2-4 week decision cycle
- Over $100,000: Professional financial advice, 30-60 day decision cycle
