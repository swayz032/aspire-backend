## Client Onboarding Checklist

A structured onboarding process reduces scope creep, sets expectations, and builds trust from day one. In 2025, service businesses with documented onboarding processes reported 34% fewer disputes and 28% higher repeat business rates.

### Pre-job onboarding steps
1. **Initial contact response** — Respond within 2 hours during business hours (industry leaders respond within 30 minutes; 78% of customers hire the first responder)
2. **Needs assessment call/visit** — Document scope, timeline expectations, budget range, access requirements, decision-maker identity
3. **Site visit/assessment** — Photos of current conditions, measurements, note hazards, identify material needs, document existing damage
4. **Written estimate/proposal** — Delivered within 48 hours of site visit (every day of delay reduces close rate by 5-7%)
5. **Contract/agreement signed** — Scope of work, payment terms, change order process, warranty terms, cancellation policy
6. **Deposit collected** — 25-50% deposit for jobs over $2,500 (standard across trades)
7. **Welcome packet/communication** — Project timeline, crew lead contact info, what to expect on day one, parking/access instructions
8. **Internal job setup** — Job created in scheduling system, materials ordered, crew assigned, permits pulled if needed

### Post-close onboarding
- Day-of confirmation text/call 24 hours before start
- Introduce crew lead to customer on arrival
- Walk the scope together before starting work
- Establish daily check-in cadence for multi-day jobs

## Job Estimation Framework

Accurate estimates are the foundation of profitability. Underestimate and you lose money; overestimate and you lose the job.

### Four-component estimation model
**Estimate = Labor + Materials + Overhead + Profit Margin**

### Labor calculation
- **Hours estimate:** Break job into tasks, estimate each task separately, add 10-15% buffer
- **Loaded labor rate:** Hourly wage x 1.35-1.50 (covers FICA, workers comp, benefits, PTO, non-billable time)
- **Example:** Journeyman plumber at $32/hour = $43-48/hour loaded cost
- **Crew efficiency factor:** 2-person crew is not 2x a 1-person crew; typical efficiency is 1.6-1.8x

### Materials calculation
- Measure twice, order with 5-10% waste factor (15% for tile, paint cutting-in)
- Use current supplier pricing, not last month's catalog
- Include delivery fees, tax, and small consumables (tape, caulk, fasteners, blades)
- Material costs rose 4-6% annually in 2024-2025; price estimates older than 30 days need verification

### Overhead allocation
- Calculate total monthly overhead, divide by total billable hours to get hourly overhead rate
- Typical overhead rate for 10-person service company: $15-30/hour per field worker
- Include: rent, insurance, vehicles, fuel, office staff, software, marketing, licenses, loan payments

### Profit margin targets by trade (2025-2026)
- Plumbing: 15-22% net margin
- Painting (residential): 12-18% net margin
- Landscaping (maintenance): 10-15% net margin
- General contracting: 8-14% net margin
- Pallet manufacturing/repair: 12-20% net margin
- HVAC: 15-25% net margin

## Quote Presentation and Win Rate Tracking

### Quote presentation best practices
- Present quotes in person or via video call when possible (increases close rate by 20-30% vs email alone)
- Use a professional template with your logo, license number, and insurance info
- Break costs into categories the customer understands (labor, materials, permits)
- Include 2-3 options when appropriate (good/better/best) — the middle option wins 60% of the time
- State what is excluded explicitly to prevent scope disputes
- Include a valid-for date (14-30 days standard)

### Win rate benchmarks and tracking
- **Target close rate:** 35-45% for residential, 25-35% for commercial
- **Below 25%:** Pricing too high, slow response time, or poor presentation
- **Above 55%:** Likely leaving money on the table — test raising prices 5-10%
- Track every quote: date sent, amount, result (won/lost/no response), reason for loss
- Review win/loss data monthly; adjust pricing and process quarterly

### Follow-up cadence for open quotes
- Day 2: Friendly check-in ("Any questions about the estimate?")
- Day 7: Value-add follow-up (share a relevant project photo or testimonial)
- Day 14: Final follow-up before expiration
- After expiration: One more touch at 30 days, then archive

## Quality Control

### Before/after documentation
- **Photograph everything** before starting work — protects against pre-existing damage claims
- Take progress photos at each major milestone
- Final photos of completed work from multiple angles
- Store photos linked to the job record in your system (not just on a phone camera roll)

### Inspection checklists by phase
1. **Pre-work inspection:** Site conditions match estimate assumptions, materials on-site and correct, safety hazards identified
2. **In-progress checkpoints:** Work matches spec at 25%, 50%, 75% completion; catch errors early
3. **Pre-customer walkthrough:** Crew lead inspects all work against scope, punch list created and completed
4. **Customer walkthrough:** Walk scope together, demonstrate work, document any concerns, get sign-off

### Quality metrics to track
- **Callback rate:** Under 3% is excellent, 3-5% is acceptable, above 5% needs immediate attention
- **First-time fix rate:** 90%+ for service/repair businesses
- **Customer satisfaction score:** Survey after every job, target 4.5+/5.0
- **Warranty claim rate:** Under 2% annually

## Vendor Management

### Vendor selection criteria
- **Price:** Within 10% of lowest bid (cheapest is rarely best)
- **Reliability:** On-time delivery rate above 95%
- **Quality:** Consistent product/material quality with low defect rate
- **Terms:** Net 30 payment terms standard; negotiate Net 45 for large accounts
- **Responsiveness:** Same-day response on urgent orders
- **Proximity:** Local suppliers reduce shipping costs and enable same-day pickup

### Negotiation tactics
- Consolidate purchases with fewer suppliers for volume pricing (10-20% discounts typical above $50K/year)
- Ask for contractor pricing tiers — most suppliers have them but do not advertise them
- Negotiate annual pricing agreements to lock in rates and protect against mid-year increases
- Request free delivery above a minimum order threshold ($500-1,000 typical)
- Pay early to build goodwill and negotiate 1-2% early payment discounts

### Performance tracking
- Track delivery accuracy and timeliness monthly
- Document quality issues with photos and dates
- Review vendor performance quarterly
- Maintain at least 2 qualified vendors for every critical material category (never be dependent on one supplier)
- Annual vendor review: renegotiate terms, compare market pricing, evaluate relationship value
