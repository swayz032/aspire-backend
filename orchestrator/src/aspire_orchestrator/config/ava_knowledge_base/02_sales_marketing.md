## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Benchmarks by Trade

CAC is the total cost to acquire one new paying customer: all marketing spend + sales labor divided by new customers gained.

### 2025-2026 CAC Ranges by Trade
- **Plumbing**: $150-$350 per customer (emergency services on the lower end, remodel jobs higher)
- **Painting (residential)**: $120-$280 per customer
- **Landscaping**: $100-$250 per customer (seasonal acquisition spikes in spring)
- **General contracting**: $300-$600 per customer (longer sales cycle, higher job value)
- **HVAC**: $200-$400 per customer
- **Electrical**: $180-$380 per customer
- **Pallet companies (B2B)**: $400-$800 per customer (fewer customers, higher lifetime value)

### LTV:CAC Ratio
- **Minimum viable ratio**: 3:1 (lifetime value should be 3x acquisition cost)
- **Healthy ratio**: 4:1 to 6:1
- **Above 6:1**: Underinvesting in growth — spend more on marketing
- **Below 3:1**: Losing money on acquisition — fix retention or reduce spend
- **Calculation**: LTV = (average job value) x (jobs per year per customer) x (average customer lifespan in years)

## Marketing Budget Guidelines

### Revenue-Based Allocation (2025-2026)
- **Established businesses (stable revenue)**: 5-10% of gross revenue on marketing
- **Growth-mode businesses (scaling up)**: 10-20% of gross revenue on marketing
- **New businesses (first 2 years)**: 15-20% of gross revenue — front-load brand awareness
- **SBA recommendation**: 7-8% of gross revenue for businesses under $5M

### Monthly Budget Benchmarks
- **Solo operator ($150K revenue)**: $625-$1,250/month
- **Small crew ($500K revenue)**: $2,000-$4,200/month
- **Mid-size ($1M+ revenue)**: $4,200-$8,300/month

## Channel Effectiveness Rankings

### Tier 1: Highest ROI Channels
1. **Google Business Profile (free)**: 56% of local service searches start on Google Maps. Optimize profile with photos, hours, service area, and categories. Businesses with complete profiles get 7x more clicks than incomplete ones.
2. **Referral programs**: Lowest CAC channel. Average referral customer has 16% higher LTV than non-referral. Cost: $25-$100 referral bonus per new customer.
3. **Email marketing**: Highest ROI of any digital channel — $36-$42 return per $1 spent (2025 DMA data). Monthly newsletters, seasonal reminders, and maintenance follow-ups.
4. **Online reviews**: 87% of consumers read online reviews for local businesses (2025 BrightLocal). Businesses with 4.5+ stars earn 28% more revenue than those with 3.5 stars.

### Tier 2: Strong Channels
5. **Google Local Service Ads (LSA)**: Pay-per-lead (not per click). $25-$75 per lead for trades. Google Guaranteed badge increases trust. 2025-2026 CPL has risen 12-18% YoY.
6. **Google Search Ads (PPC)**: $3-$12 CPC for trade keywords in 2025-2026. "Plumber near me" averages $8.50 CPC. Budget $500-$2,000/month minimum.
7. **Nextdoor**: Free business page. 1 in 4 US households active. Strong for neighborhood-based services.
8. **Facebook/Instagram**: $1.50-$4.00 CPC for local service ads. Before/after project photos perform best. Budget: $300-$800/month for local targeting.

### Tier 3: Supplemental Channels
9. **Yard signs and vehicle wraps**: Vehicle wraps generate 30,000-70,000 daily impressions. Cost: $2,500-$5,000 for full wrap, lasts 5-7 years.
10. **Home Advisor / Angi / Thumbtack**: $15-$80 per lead depending on trade. Quality varies. Best as supplement, not primary channel.
11. **Direct mail**: 4.4% response rate for house-list mailings (2025 ANA data). $0.50-$1.50 per piece. Effective for seasonal pushes.

## Customer Retention Benchmarks

### Service Business Retention Rates
- **Average return rate**: 40-60% for service businesses (one-time jobs like painting are lower; recurring like landscaping are higher)
- **Top performers**: 70-80% retention with active follow-up systems
- **CRM impact**: 27% improvement in customer retention when using CRM consistently (Salesforce 2025 SMB report)
- **Follow-up timing**: Contact within 48 hours of job completion for review request. 30-day follow-up for satisfaction check. 6-12 month follow-up for maintenance/repeat service.

### Retention Tactics That Work
- **Maintenance agreements**: Offer annual maintenance contracts (HVAC, plumbing, landscaping). Converts one-time customers to recurring revenue. 60-80% renewal rate on maintenance contracts.
- **Birthday/anniversary emails**: Simple automated touch that keeps brand top-of-mind
- **Seasonal reminders**: "Time to winterize your irrigation" or "Spring AC tune-up" — drives repeat bookings
- **Loyalty discounts**: 5-10% repeat customer discount. Cost is far less than new customer acquisition.

## Review Management Strategy

### Review Volume Targets
- **Minimum credible**: 20+ reviews on Google
- **Competitive**: 50-100+ reviews
- **Dominant local presence**: 200+ reviews
- **Response rate**: Respond to 100% of reviews (positive and negative) within 24 hours

### How to Get Reviews
- Ask every customer at job completion (in person converts 70% of the time vs 10% for email-only requests)
- Send automated text/email within 2 hours of job completion with direct Google review link
- Never offer incentives for reviews (violates Google and FTC guidelines)
- Negative review response framework: Acknowledge, apologize, take offline ("Please call us at...")

## Referral Program Design

### Structure
- **Customer referral bonus**: $25-$100 cash, gift card, or service credit per successful referral
- **Trigger**: Paid when referred customer completes first job (not just books)
- **Communication**: Mention at job completion, include in follow-up email, print referral cards
- **Tracking**: Use unique referral codes or "How did you hear about us?" on every intake

### Benchmarks
- Well-run referral programs generate 15-25% of new customers
- Referred customers close at 3-5x the rate of cold leads
- Average program cost: $50-$75 per acquired customer (vs $150-$400 for paid ads)

## Local SEO Strategy (2025-2026)

### Google Business Profile Optimization
- Complete every field: business name, categories (primary + secondary), service area, hours, attributes
- Add 10+ high-quality photos (team, vehicles, completed work). Update monthly.
- Post weekly Google Business updates (offers, project highlights, tips)
- Ensure NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone) across all directories

### Website SEO for Trades
- Create service-area pages: "Plumbing Services in [City Name]" for each city served
- Target long-tail keywords: "emergency plumber [city]", "[trade] near me", "[trade] cost [city]"
- Page speed matters: 53% of mobile visitors leave if page takes over 3 seconds (Google 2025)
- Mobile-first: 68% of local service searches happen on mobile devices

### Citation Building
- Ensure listings on: Google, Yelp, BBB, Angi, Facebook, Apple Maps, Bing Places
- Industry-specific directories: HomeAdvisor, Houzz, BuildZoom, Porch
- Consistent NAP across all 15-20 directories. Inconsistencies hurt rankings.

### Social Media ROI for Trades
- **Best platforms**: Facebook (broad reach), Instagram (visual before/after), YouTube (how-to builds authority), TikTok (younger homeowner demographic, growing fast)
