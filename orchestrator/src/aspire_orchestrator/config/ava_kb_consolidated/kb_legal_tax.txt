# Legal and Tax Guide for Small Service Businesses

Priority: B | Last Updated: 2026-03 | Sources: 2025-2026 IRS, SBA, state regulatory data

## Entity Selection: LLC vs S-Corp

### Sole Proprietorship (Starting Point)
- Default for single-owner businesses with no entity filing
- All income flows to personal return (Schedule C)
- Owner pays self-employment tax on 100% of net profit: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)
- No liability protection. Personal assets are exposed.
- Acceptable only for very early stage or side-hustle income under $30,000/year

### LLC (Limited Liability Company)
- **Liability protection**: Personal assets shielded from business debts and lawsuits
- **Tax treatment**: By default, taxed as sole proprietorship (single member) or partnership (multi-member)
- **Self-employment tax**: Still owed on all net profit (same as sole prop) unless S-Corp election is made
- **Filing cost**: $50-$500 depending on state. Annual report fees: $0-$300/year.
- **Best for**: Businesses earning under $50,000-$60,000 net profit, or those wanting simplicity

### S-Corp Election (LLC taxed as S-Corp)
- **How it works**: File IRS Form 2553. LLC remains an LLC legally, but is taxed as an S-Corp.
- **Key benefit**: Split income into salary (subject to FICA) and distributions (not subject to FICA). Saves 15.3% self-employment tax on the distribution portion.
- **Reasonable salary requirement**: IRS requires owner to pay themselves a "reasonable salary" before taking distributions. Underpaying salary is the #1 audit trigger.

### When S-Corp Election Makes Sense
- **Net profit consistently above $60,000-$80,000/year** (below this, the administrative costs outweigh savings)
- **Example at $120,000 net profit**:
  - Sole prop/LLC: Self-employment tax on $120,000 = ~$17,000
  - S-Corp with $70,000 salary + $50,000 distribution: FICA on $70,000 = ~$10,700. Savings: ~$6,300/year
- **Added costs of S-Corp**: Payroll processing ($50-$150/month), additional tax return (Form 1120-S: $500-$1,500 to prepare), payroll tax filings
- **Break-even**: S-Corp saves money when annual tax savings exceed $2,000-$4,000 in added admin costs

### 2025-2026 Thresholds
- **Social Security wage base (2025)**: $176,100 (only earnings up to this cap are subject to 12.4% Social Security tax)
- **Medicare**: No cap — 2.9% on all earnings. Additional 0.9% Medicare surtax on earnings above $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (married filing jointly)
- **S-Corp reasonable salary benchmarks**: IRS looks at comparable wages for the role, hours worked, and revenue. For a plumber-owner doing 30+ hours of field work: $55,000-$85,000 is a common reasonable salary range.

## Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction

### The 20% Pass-Through Deduction (Section 199A)
- **What it is**: Owners of pass-through entities (sole props, LLCs, S-Corps, partnerships) can deduct 20% of qualified business income from their taxable income
- **Status**: Made permanent under the 2025 tax legislation (previously set to expire after 2025)
- **Effect**: Reduces effective tax rate by approximately 4-7 percentage points for qualifying businesses

### Eligibility
- **Under $191,950 taxable income (single) / $383,900 (married filing jointly) in 2025**: Full 20% deduction with no restrictions
- **Above these thresholds**: Deduction phases out for "specified service trades" (accounting, law, consulting — but NOT construction, plumbing, painting, landscaping, or other trades)
- **Trade businesses are NOT specified service trades**: Plumbers, painters, electricians, landscapers, and contractors qualify for the full QBI deduction regardless of income level (subject to W-2 wage/capital limitations at higher incomes)

### Calculation Example
- Business net income: $100,000
- QBI deduction: $20,000 (20% of $100,000)
- Taxable income reduced to $80,000
- At 22% marginal rate: saves $4,400 in federal tax

## Trade-Specific Licensing and Bonding

### Licensing Requirements (Varies by State)
- **Plumbing**: Licensed in all 50 states. Typically requires journeyman license (4-5 years apprenticeship + exam) and master plumber license (additional 2-3 years + exam). Contractor license for running a business.
- **Electrical**: Licensed in all 50 states. Journeyman (4 years + exam), Master Electrician (additional experience + exam), Electrical Contractor license.
- **HVAC**: Licensed in most states. EPA Section 608 certification required for refrigerant handling (federal). State contractor license.
- **General contracting**: 35+ states require contractor licenses. Thresholds vary: some states license at $0 (any work), others at $5,000-$30,000+ project value.
- **Painting**: Fewer states require painting-specific licenses, but EPA RRP (Renovation, Repair, Painting) certification is required for work on pre-1978 buildings (lead paint). General contractor license may apply above certain dollar thresholds.
- **Landscaping**: Pesticide applicator license required for chemical treatments (all states). Irrigation licenses in many states. General contractor license for hardscape above threshold amounts.

### Bonding Requirements
- **Surety bond**: Required in most states for licensed contractors. Protects customers if contractor fails to complete work or violates regulations.
- **Bond amounts**: $5,000-$25,000 for most trade licenses (varies by state and license type)
- **Cost**: 1-5% of bond face value annually. A $15,000 bond costs $150-$750/year depending on credit score.
- **Performance bonds**: Required for government/commercial contracts. Typically 100% of contract value. Cost: 1-3% of contract value.

## Insurance Requirements

### Required Coverage
| Type | What It Covers | Typical Cost (2025-2026) |
|------|---------------|------------------------|
| General liability | Third-party injury, property damage | $500-$2,500/year per $1M coverage |
| Workers compensation | Employee injuries on the job | $2-$15 per $100 of payroll (varies by trade and state) |
| Commercial auto | Business vehicle accidents | $1,800-$4,500/year per vehicle |
| Professional liability (E&O) | Errors in professional advice/work | $500-$2,000/year |

### Recommended Additional Coverage
- **Inland marine/tools coverage**: Covers tools and equipment in transit or on job sites. $300-$800/year for $50,000 coverage.
- **Umbrella policy**: Additional $1-$5M liability above primary policies. $300-$800/year per $1M.
- **Cyber liability**: If storing customer data digitally. $500-$1,500/year.
- **Builder's risk**: For construction/renovation projects. Covers materials and work in progress. Project-specific pricing.

### Workers Comp Rates by Trade (2025-2026, per $100 of payroll)
- **Plumbing**: $3.50-$7.00
- **Electrical**: $3.00-$6.50
- **HVAC**: $3.00-$5.50
- **Roofing**: $10.00-$25.00 (highest risk)
- **Painting**: $4.00-$8.00
- **Landscaping**: $4.00-$9.00
- **Carpentry**: $6.00-$12.00
- **Note**: Rates vary significantly by state and claims history. Experience modification rate (EMR) above 1.0 increases premiums; below 1.0 decreases them.

## Quarterly Estimated Tax Payments

### Who Must Pay
- Any business owner who expects to owe $1,000+ in federal tax for the year
- Penalty for underpayment: currently ~8% annually on the shortfall (based on federal interest rate)

### Due Dates (2025-2026)
- Q1: April 15
- Q2: June 15
- Q3: September 15
- Q4: January 15 (of the following year)

### Safe Harbor Rules
- Pay 100% of prior year's tax liability in equal quarterly installments (110% if AGI was above $150,000)
- OR pay 90% of current year's expected liability
- Meeting either safe harbor avoids underpayment penalties regardless of actual tax owed

### Tax Deduction Strategies for Trades
- **Section 179 expensing (2025)**: Deduct up to $1,250,000 of equipment purchases in the year bought (vehicles, tools, equipment). Phase-out begins at $3,130,000 total purchases.
- **Bonus depreciation (2025)**: 40% first-year bonus depreciation on new and used equipment (phasing down from 100% in 2022: 80% in 2023, 60% in 2024, 40% in 2025, 20% in 2026).
- **Vehicle deduction**: Heavy vehicles (GVWR over 6,000 lbs) qualify for full Section 179 deduction. Ford F-250, RAM 2500, Sprinter vans, and most service trucks qualify.
- **Home office deduction**: Simplified method: $5/sq ft, up to 300 sq ft ($1,500 max). Regular method: actual expenses prorated by square footage. Must be exclusive and regular use.
- **Retirement contributions**: SEP-IRA allows up to 25% of net self-employment income (max $70,000 in 2025). Solo 401(k) allows $23,500 employee contribution + 25% employer contribution.

## 2026 Tax Changes and New Reporting Rules

### $600 Platform Reporting Threshold (Form 1099-K)
- **Effective 2025 tax year (reported in early 2026)**: Payment platforms (Venmo, PayPal, Zelle business, Square, Stripe) must report transactions totaling $600+ to the IRS via Form 1099-K
- **Previous threshold**: $20,000 AND 200 transactions
- **Impact**: Businesses receiving payments through these platforms will receive 1099-K forms. Ensure business income tracking matches platform records.
- **Action item**: Separate personal and business payment accounts. Track all platform income meticulously.

### Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) Report
- **Requirement**: Most LLCs and corporations must file BOI reports with FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)
- **Deadline**: New entities formed in 2025-2026 must file within 90 days of formation. Existing entities that were required to report must already be filed.
- **Penalty for non-filing**: Up to $500/day, plus criminal penalties up to $10,000 and 2 years imprisonment
- **Note**: Legal challenges have created uncertainty around enforcement — check current status with an attorney before assuming non-applicability.

## Contracts and Collections

### Mechanic's Lien Rights
- **What it is**: Legal claim against a property for unpaid work or materials. Most powerful collection tool for contractors.
- **Availability**: All 50 states have mechanic's lien statutes, but rules vary significantly
- **Typical requirements**: Preliminary notice (required in most states, within 20-30 days of starting work), lien filing (60-120 days after completion), enforcement action (6-12 months after filing)
- **Critical**: Missing a deadline by even one day can void lien rights. Track deadlines for every project.

### Contract Essentials for Service Businesses
Every job over $500 needs a written contract including:
1. Scope of work (specific deliverables, not vague descriptions)
2. Price (fixed bid or T&M with not-to-exceed cap)
3. Payment terms (deposit, progress payments, final payment)
4. Change order process (written approval required for scope changes)
5. Timeline (start date, completion date, delay provisions)
6. Warranty terms (1-year workmanship warranty is standard in trades)
7. Dispute resolution (mediation before litigation saves money)
8. Cancellation/termination terms

### Small Claims Court
- **Limits**: $2,500-$25,000 depending on state (most are $5,000-$10,000)
- **No attorney needed**: Designed for self-representation
- **Filing fee**: $30-$200
- **Timeline**: 30-60 days from filing to hearing in most jurisdictions
- **Best for**: Unpaid invoices under the state limit where a mechanic's lien is not available or has expired
