# Trade-Specific Playbooks

## Plumbing

### Pricing and Revenue
Standard hourly rates (2025-2026): $85-$200/hr depending on market and specialization. Master plumber rates run 25-40% higher than journeyman rates. Emergency and after-hours calls: $150-$350/hr with a minimum dispatch fee of $75-$150.

Average order value (AOV): $300-$500 for residential service calls. Water heater replacements: $1,200-$3,500 (tank) or $3,500-$6,500 (tankless). Sewer line repair/replacement: $3,000-$15,000. Bathroom remodel rough-in: $2,500-$7,000.

Revenue per technician benchmark: $200,000-$300,000 annually. Top-performing plumbing companies achieve $300,000-$350,000 per tech by combining high average tickets with 5-6 completed calls per day. Minimum viable: $180,000/tech/year to cover fully burdened labor costs plus overhead allocation.

### Service Mix Optimization
Target service mix for maximum profitability: 60% service/repair (highest margin at 55-65% gross), 25% replacements (moderate margin at 35-45% gross), 15% new construction (lowest margin at 20-30% gross but provides volume).

Maintenance agreement penetration: Target 15-25% of residential customers on annual agreements ($150-$300/year). These customers spend 2.3x more annually than non-agreement customers and have 85% retention rates vs. 40% for one-time customers.

Drain cleaning as a gateway: $150-$350 per call, high frequency, and 30-40% of drain calls convert to larger plumbing repairs within 12 months. Top shops use drain cleaning as their primary customer acquisition channel.

### Key Metrics
- Callback rate: Target under 2% (industry average 3-5%)
- Average drive time: Under 25 minutes per call
- First-call completion: 85%+ with properly stocked trucks
- Material markup: 50-65% over wholesale (do not go below 40%)

## Painting

### Pricing Structure
Interior painting rates (2025-2026): $2-$6 per square foot of wall area. National average: $3.50/sq ft. Premium finishes (faux, venetian plaster, specialty textures): $8-$15/sq ft.

Exterior painting rates: $1.50-$4 per square foot of paintable surface. Average 2,000 sq ft home exterior: $4,000-$8,000 total. Pressure washing add-on: $0.25-$0.50/sq ft, 85%+ margin.

Room-based pricing (popular with homeowners): Standard bedroom $350-$750, living room $600-$1,500, kitchen $400-$1,000, bathroom $250-$600. These are total prices including 2 coats, standard prep, and materials.

### Crew Efficiency Metrics
Interior production rate: 200-400 sq ft per painter per hour (walls, standard prep, 2 coats). A 2-person crew completes a standard bedroom (400 sq ft wall area) in 3-5 hours including prep, prime, 2 coats, and cleanup.

Exterior production rate: 100-250 sq ft per painter per hour depending on surface condition, height, and access. Scaffolding or lift rental adds $200-$800/day but increases production by 40-60% vs. ladder work.

Labor as percentage of job cost: 70-85% for painting (highest labor ratio of any trade). Material costs represent only 15-25% of job price. A gallon of premium paint ($40-$70) covers 350-400 sq ft, making material cost $0.10-$0.20/sq ft.

Crew utilization: Target 75-85% billable hours. Weather days reduce exterior crew utilization by 10-20% annually. Smart scheduling buffers interior work for weather days.

## HVAC

### Margins and Pricing
Gross margin: 30-45% on service/repair, 20-30% on equipment installation. Net profit: 12-20% for well-run operations. Diagnostic fee: $75-$150, waived with repair (converts 70-80% of diagnostics into repairs).

Equipment installation pricing: Residential system replacement $5,000-$15,000 depending on system size (2-5 ton), efficiency rating, and ductwork modifications. High-efficiency systems (18+ SEER) command $2,000-$4,000 premium over standard (14-16 SEER). The 2025 SEER2 minimum efficiency standards increased baseline equipment costs by 8-12%.

### Maintenance Contracts
Residential maintenance agreements: $150-$500/year for 1-2 visits. Commercial: $1,200-$5,000/year per unit. Maintenance contracts generate 15-25% of total revenue but contribute 30-40% of gross profit due to predictable scheduling and lower customer acquisition cost.

Agreement retention rate benchmark: 80-90% annually. Below 75% indicates service quality or pricing issues. Each agreement customer generates $3,200-$5,500 in lifetime value over a 7-10 year equipment cycle.

### Seasonal Management
Revenue distribution: In southern markets, 55-65% of annual revenue occurs May through September. January-February can be 30-50% of monthly average. Cash reserve recommendation: 2-3 months of fixed overhead to bridge the slow season.

Counter-seasonal strategies: Indoor air quality services (UV lights, air purifiers, duct cleaning) in winter months. Energy audit upsells: $200-$500 per assessment, 40% conversion to efficiency upgrades. These strategies can increase winter revenue by 25-40%.

## Landscaping

### Revenue and Pricing
Revenue per customer (full-service maintenance): $14,682 annually for comprehensive maintenance clients including mowing, edging, seasonal planting, mulching, and irrigation management.

Mowing rates: $30-$80 per visit for residential (average lot), $150-$500+ per visit for commercial properties. Per-acre rate: $40-$80 for open mowing, $80-$150 for detailed landscaping areas.

Recurring vs. one-time revenue: Healthy landscaping businesses derive 60-70% of revenue from recurring maintenance contracts and 30-40% from project/installation work. Maintenance provides cash flow stability; installations provide margin (35-45% gross on hardscaping).

### Crew Economics
Crew of 3 (1 foreman + 2 laborers): $800-$1,200/day in billings for maintenance, $1,500-$3,000/day for installation/hardscaping. Target: 8-12 residential maintenance stops per crew per day. Each stop averages 30-45 minutes including travel.

Labor cost per crew-hour: $75-$120 fully burdened (wages + taxes + benefits + workers comp). Equipment cost per crew-hour: $15-$30 including depreciation, fuel, and maintenance. Target billing rate: 2.5-3x total crew cost.

Seasonal workforce: 40-60% of landscaping labor in southern markets is seasonal (March-November). H-2B visa workers cost $14-$18/hr plus $2,000-$4,000 per worker in visa and housing costs. Retention of seasonal crews year-over-year: target 70%+.

## Pallet and Warehouse Services

### Pallet Economics
New GMA pallet (48x40): $12-$18. Recycled/repaired: $5-$9. Heat-treated (ISPM-15): $15-$22. Plastic reusable: $40-$120 (80-100+ trip lifecycle vs. 15-20 for wood).

Pallet recycling and repair business: Buy damaged pallets at $1-$3 each, repair for $2-$4 in labor/materials, sell at $5-$9. Gross margin: 40-55% on repair operations. Volume target for viability: 500+ pallets/week.

Utilization benchmark: 75-90% of pallet pool should be in active circulation at any time. Below 70% indicates excess inventory tying up capital. Above 90% risks stockouts during demand spikes.

GMA spec compliance: 4-way entry, 2,500 lb racking capacity minimum, no broken stringers, no protruding nails. Rejection rate at major retailers: 5-15% of incoming pallets fail inspection.

### Warehouse Service Pricing
Pallet storage: $8-$12/position/month (national average). Handling in/out: $3-$6 per pallet per movement. Pick and pack: $2-$5 per order line. Cross-docking: $4-$8 per pallet. Value-added services (labeling, kitting, shrink-wrapping): $0.25-$2.00 per unit.

## General Contractors

### Margin Structure
Gross margin target: 25-40%. Residential remodeling: 30-40%. Commercial tenant buildout: 25-35%. New residential construction: 18-25%. Custom homes: 20-30%.

Net profit: 5-12% after all overhead. Industry average is 6-8%. Companies consistently above 10% net are top-decile performers.

### Subcontractor Management
Subcontractor markup: 10-20% on sub costs is standard. Below 10% does not cover coordination and warranty risk. Maintain 2-3 qualified subs per trade. Payment terms to subs: Net 30 from owner payment (pay-when-paid clauses).

Change order management: 15-25% of project revenue on remodeling projects comes from change orders. Markup on changes: 25-50% (higher than base contract work due to disruption costs). Document every change with signed authorization before work begins.

### Project Management Metrics
- Schedule variance: Target within 5% of planned duration
- Cost variance: Target within 3% of budget
- RFI response time: Under 48 hours to prevent schedule impact
- Punch list items per $100K of contract value: Under 15 items
- Warranty callback rate: Under 3% of completed projects within first year
