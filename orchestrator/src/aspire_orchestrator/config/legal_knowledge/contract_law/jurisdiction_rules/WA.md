# Washington State Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Washington adopted UETA (RCW 1.80, effective 2020 — later than most states). Prior to 2020, Washington had its own Electronic Authentication Act (RCW 19.34).

**Specific rules:**
- Electronic signatures have full legal equivalence under both UETA and federal ESIGN
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under RCW 42.45 (effective 2020, initially for COVID; made permanent)
- Washington courts have upheld e-signatures in employment, real estate, and commercial contexts

## Non-Compete Agreements
Washington enacted strong restrictions on non-competes under RCW 49.62 (effective January 1, 2020).

**Key restrictions:**
- Non-competes VOID for employees earning less than $116,594/year (adjusted annually for inflation — check current threshold)
- Non-competes VOID for independent contractors earning less than $291,486/year
- Maximum duration: 18 months (presumptive; court may allow longer if employer proves necessary)
- Employer must disclose non-compete terms by the earlier of: acceptance of the offer or 10 business days before start of employment
- For existing employees, non-compete must be supported by independent consideration (bonus, raise, promotion, new term of employment)
- If employee is laid off, non-compete is enforceable ONLY if employer pays base salary during the restricted period (garden leave)
- Prevailing employee in enforcement action recovers actual damages, statutory damages of $5,000 OR actual damages (whichever is greater), and attorney fees

**Franchisor non-competes:** Void against franchisees under RCW 49.62.060 unless the franchisee is selling the franchise.

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 6 years (RCW 4.16.040)
- **Oral contracts:** 3 years (RCW 4.16.080)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC)
- **Fraud:** 3 years from discovery (RCW 4.16.080)
- **Professional malpractice:** 3 years from act, or 1 year from discovery (RCW 4.16.350)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 20 days by tenant; 60 days by landlord (RCW 59.18.200)
- **Rent increase:** 60 days' written notice (RCW 59.18.140)
- **Eviction (non-payment):** 14-day notice (RCW 59.12.030 — increased from 3 days under pandemic legislation, check current status)
- **Lease violation (other):** 10-day notice to comply or vacate

## Washington-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** No statutory limit
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after tenant vacates (RCW 59.18.280)
- **Itemized deductions required** with full statement
- **Checklist required:** Landlord must provide detailed checklist of condition at move-in; failure to provide checklist prevents deduction from deposit for pre-existing conditions
- **Interest:** Not required
- **Penalty:** Failure to return within 30 days: landlord liable for 2x deposit amount plus attorney fees

### Contractor Licensing
Washington requires contractor registration through the Department of Labor & Industries (L&I):
- **General Contractor license:** Required for all work regardless of value
- **Specialty Contractor license:** Specific trades (electrical, plumbing, HVAC)
- **Must carry surety bond** ($12,000 minimum for general; varies by specialty)
- **Insurance:** Must carry liability insurance and workers' compensation
- **Unlicensed work:** Gross misdemeanor; cannot file lien; cannot sue for payment; must refund payments if demanded by consumer

### Mechanic's Lien (RCW 60.04)
- **Pre-claim notice:** Required for all potential lien claimants EXCEPT the GC — must be given within 60 days of first furnishing (RCW 60.04.031)
- **Lien filing deadline:** 90 days from completion, cessation of work, or furnishing of materials (RCW 60.04.091)
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 8 months from filing (RCW 60.04.141)
- **Residential projects:** Additional notice requirements for projects on owner-occupied residences (4+ units exempt)

### Consumer Protection Act (RCW 19.86)
- Modeled on FTC Act (Section 5)
- Prohibits unfair or deceptive acts in trade/commerce
- Private right of action for actual damages, treble damages (up to $25,000), and attorney fees
- Applies to both consumer and business-to-business transactions
- No privity requirement
- Injunctive relief available

### Minimum Wage Impact on Contractor Classification
Washington has strict worker classification rules (ABC test for some purposes). Misclassification penalties are significant. Clara should flag contractor agreements that exhibit employment characteristics under Washington's stricter classification standards.
