# Florida Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Florida adopted UETA (Florida Statutes Chapter 668, Part II, effective 2000) and also enacted the Florida Electronic Signature Act (668.001-668.006).

**Specific rules:**
- Electronic signatures have full legal effect
- Florida was an early e-signature adopter with its own statute predating UETA
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under Florida Statutes 117.265 (effective January 2020)
- Florida notaries can perform RON for signers located anywhere (not just Florida residents)

## Non-Compete Agreements
Florida enforces non-competes under Florida Statute 542.335, which is considered one of the most employer-friendly statutes in the country.

**Enforceability framework:**
- Must protect a legitimate business interest (trade secrets, valuable confidential information, substantial client relationships, customer goodwill, extraordinary or specialized training)
- Presumptively reasonable durations: 6 months (non-compete tied to former employee); 2 years (non-compete tied to sale of business)
- Presumptively unreasonable: Over 2 years for employees; over 3 years for sale of business
- Court MUST enforce if reasonable — cannot refuse enforcement on public policy grounds
- Court MUST NOT consider economic hardship on the person subject to the restraint

**Florida-specific advantage for employers:**
- Rebuttable presumption of irreparable injury when valid non-compete is violated (easier to get injunction)
- Courts will blue-pencil overly broad covenants

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 5 years (Florida Statutes 95.11(2)(b))
- **Oral contracts:** 4 years (Florida Statutes 95.11(3)(k))
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC, Florida Statutes 672.725)
- **Fraud:** 4 years from discovery (Florida Statutes 95.031(2)(a))
- **Professional malpractice (legal):** 2 years (Florida Statutes 95.11(4)(a))

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 15 days (Florida Statutes 83.57)
- **Commercial lease default:** Per lease terms; Florida Statutes 83.20 governs nonresidential tenancies
- **Eviction (residential):** 3-day notice for nonpayment; 7-day notice for lease violation (curable); 7-day unconditional quit for repeated violations

## Florida-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** No statutory limit
- **Return deadline:** 15 days if no deductions; 30 days with itemized claim letter if deductions (Florida Statutes 83.49)
- **Tenant response:** Tenant has 15 days to object to claimed deductions; if no objection, landlord may deduct
- **Holding requirements:** Must be held in a Florida banking institution; landlord must notify tenant of bank name/address within 30 days
- **Interest option:** Landlord may post surety bond instead of holding deposit
- **Penalty:** Bad faith retention: actual damages + court costs

### Contractor Licensing
Florida requires contractor licensing through the DBPR (Department of Business and Professional Regulation):
- **Certified contractors:** State-wide license, issued by Construction Industry Licensing Board
- **Registered contractors:** County-level license, registered with state
- **Categories:** General, building, residential, mechanical, roofing, electrical, plumbing, sheet metal, pool, solar, pollutant storage
- **Unlicensed contracting:** Third-degree felony for contracting without a license (Florida Statutes 489.127)

### Construction Lien Law (Chapter 713)
- **Notice to Owner (NTO):** Required within 45 days of first furnishing labor/materials
- **Contractor's Final Affidavit:** Required before final payment
- **Claim of Lien filing:** Within 90 days of last furnishing
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 1 year from recording
- **Notice of Contest of Lien:** Owner can file to shorten foreclosure deadline to 60 days
- **Statutory payment bond:** Can be used to transfer lien from property to bond

### Homestead Exemption
Florida's homestead exemption is one of the strongest in the country (unlimited value, with acreage limits). Affects enforceability of judgments from contract disputes — judgment creditors generally cannot force sale of homestead property.

### Business Entity Formation
Florida is a popular state for LLC formation:
- No state income tax (no corporate or personal income tax)
- Annual report required ($138.75 for LLCs)
- Charging order protection for single-member LLCs (Florida Statutes 605.0503)
