# New Jersey Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
New Jersey adopted UETA (NJSA 12A:12-1 through 12A:12-24, effective 2001). Electronic signatures have full legal equivalence.

**Specific rules:**
- New Jersey permits remote online notarization (RON) under Executive Order 171 (2020, COVID emergency), extended and made permanent
- Courts have upheld e-signatures in employment agreements, real estate contracts, and arbitration agreements

## Non-Compete Agreements
New Jersey enforces non-competes under common law reasonableness analysis. No specific statute, though legislation has been proposed multiple times.

**Enforceability factors (Solari Industries test):**
- Must protect a legitimate business interest
- Must not impose undue hardship on the employee
- Must not be injurious to the public
- Must be reasonable in temporal and geographic scope
- Consideration: Employment is sufficient for new hires; continued employment plus additional consideration may be needed for existing employees (inconsistent case law)

**New Jersey-specific:**
- Courts will blue-pencil overly broad covenants
- New Jersey courts tend to be more employee-friendly than many states
- Courts consider whether the employee was terminated without cause — non-competes may not be enforced against involuntarily terminated employees (Hogan v. Bergen Brunswig Corp. line of cases)
- Garden leave provisions increase enforceability

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 6 years (NJSA 2A:14-1)
- **Oral contracts:** 6 years (NJSA 2A:14-1)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC)
- **Fraud:** 6 years from discovery (NJSA 2A:14-1)
- **Professional malpractice:** 6 years from act (NJSA 2A:14-1), or discovery rule

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 1 month (NJSA 2A:18-56)
- **Eviction:** Must be for cause — New Jersey does NOT allow no-cause evictions under the Anti-Eviction Act (NJSA 2A:18-61.1 et seq.)
- **Senior tenants (62+) and disabled:** Additional protections; can only be evicted with government relocation assistance in certain cases

## New Jersey-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** 1.5 months' rent (NJSA 46:8-21.2)
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after lease termination; 5 days if due to fire/flood/condemnation (NJSA 46:8-21.1)
- **Itemized deductions required** with written notice
- **Interest required:** Must be held in interest-bearing account in NJ banking institution; annual statement of interest must be provided to tenant; tenant may apply interest against rent
- **Separate trust account required** — cannot commingle with landlord's personal funds

### Contractor Licensing
New Jersey requires contractor registration under the Contractor Registration Act (NJSA 56:8-136 et seq.):
- All home improvement contractors must register with the NJ Division of Consumer Affairs
- Registration number must appear on contracts and advertisements
- Separate licenses: Electrical (Board of Examiners of Electrical Contractors), Plumbing (Board of Master Plumbers), HVAC (tied to plumbing/electrical depending on trade)
- Home improvement contracts >$500 must be in writing
- Failure to register: Cannot maintain suit for collection of compensation

### Mechanic's Lien (Construction Lien Law, NJSA 2A:44A-1 et seq.)
- **Notice of unpaid balance (NUB):** Required — must serve on owner within 90 days of last furnishing (NJSA 2A:44A-6)
- **Lien filing deadline:** 90 days after last furnishing, but NUB is the critical prerequisite
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 1 year from lien filing
- **Arbitration:** New Jersey Construction Lien Law allows owner to require arbitration of lien claims

### Consumer Fraud Act (NJSA 56:8-1 et seq.)
- One of the broadest consumer protection statutes in the US
- Applies to unconscionable commercial practices, deception, fraud in connection with sale of goods/services
- Treble damages mandatory for violations (not discretionary)
- Attorney fees recoverable
- No intent requirement — strict liability
- Applies to businesses and consumers

### New Jersey Anti-Eviction Act
Unique in the US: Residential tenants cannot be evicted except for specific statutory causes (non-payment, damage, disturbance, owner occupancy, etc.). Landlord cannot refuse to renew lease without statutory cause. This fundamentally changes the lease termination dynamics compared to most other states.

### Truth in Consumer Contract, Warranty and Notice Act (NJSA 56:12-14 et seq.)
Prohibits unconscionable provisions in consumer contracts, including:
- Waiving consumer rights under state/federal law
- Shifting burden of proof
- Limiting time for consumer to file claims below statutory period
