# Illinois Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Illinois adopted UETA (815 ILCS 333, effective 2002). Electronic signatures are legally equivalent to wet-ink signatures.

**Specific rules:**
- Illinois Electronic Commerce Security Act (5 ILCS 175) provides additional framework for secure electronic transactions
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under the RON Act (effective June 2022)
- Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA) may affect some electronic authentication methods using fingerprint/facial recognition

## Non-Compete Agreements
Illinois enacted the Illinois Freedom to Work Act (820 ILCS 90, effective January 1, 2022), significantly restricting non-competes.

**Key restrictions:**
- Non-competes VOID for employees earning less than $75,000/year (threshold increases $5K every 5 years)
- Non-solicitation agreements VOID for employees earning less than $45,000/year
- Employer must advise employee in writing to consult an attorney before signing
- Employee must have at least 14 calendar days to review before signing
- Continued employment alone is NOT adequate consideration — requires additional consideration (2 years of employment post-signing, or additional monetary consideration)
- Cannot be used against employees terminated due to COVID-19 or similar circumstances

**Enforceability factors (for qualifying employees):**
- Must protect a legitimate business interest
- Must be reasonable in time (typically 1-2 years), geographic area, and scope
- Must be ancillary to a valid employment relationship
- Consideration beyond mere continued employment

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 10 years (735 ILCS 5/13-206) — one of the longest in the US
- **Oral contracts:** 5 years (735 ILCS 5/13-205)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC)
- **Fraud:** 5 years (735 ILCS 5/13-205)
- **Professional malpractice (legal):** 2 years from discovery, max 6 years from act (735 ILCS 5/13-214.3)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 30 days (735 ILCS 5/9-207)
- **Week-to-week tenancy:** 7 days
- **Eviction (non-payment):** 5-day notice (735 ILCS 5/9-209)
- **Lease violation (other):** 10-day notice to cure (735 ILCS 5/9-210)

## Illinois-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** No statewide limit (Chicago limits: 1.5x monthly rent for non-refundable move-in fees)
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after tenant vacates (for units outside Chicago); 45 days inside Chicago
- **Itemized statement required** with paid receipts or estimates
- **Interest:** Required in buildings with 25+ units (765 ILCS 710) — interest rate set annually by City Comptroller in Chicago
- **Penalty (Chicago):** 2x deposit for violation of Chicago RLTO

### Chicago RLTO (Residential Landlord Tenant Ordinance)
Chicago has its own landlord-tenant ordinance with stricter rules:
- Summary of rights must be attached to every lease
- Late fee cap: $10/month for first $500 rent; 5% of monthly rent for amounts over $500
- Lock-out prohibition with specific remedies
- Strict security deposit receipt and interest requirements
- Tenant has right to terminate lease and recover damages for RLTO violations

### Contractor Licensing
Illinois does not have a statewide general contractor license. However:
- Roofing contractors: Statewide license required (225 ILCS 335)
- Plumbers: Licensed by IL DFPR
- Electricians: Licensed by IL DFPR
- Chicago requires general contractor registration with the City
- Other municipalities may have additional requirements

### Mechanic's Lien (770 ILCS 60)
- **Preliminary notice:** Subcontractors/suppliers must serve written notice within 90 days of last furnishing
- **Lien filing deadline:** 4 months from completion for subs/suppliers; 2 years for GC
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 2 years from filing
- **Sworn statement:** Subcontractors can require GC to provide sworn statement of amounts owed

### Consumer Fraud and Deceptive Business Practices Act (815 ILCS 505)
Broad consumer protection statute:
- Prohibits unfair or deceptive acts in trade/commerce
- Private right of action with actual damages, punitive damages, and attorney fees
- No privity of contract required

### Right to Cure Act (Construction)
Illinois Construction Contract Indemnification for Negligence Act (740 ILCS 35) voids indemnification clauses in construction contracts that attempt to indemnify a party for its own negligence.
