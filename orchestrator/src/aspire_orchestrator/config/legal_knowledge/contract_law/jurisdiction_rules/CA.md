# California Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
California adopted UETA (Civil Code 1633.1-1633.17) and recognizes electronic signatures as equivalent to wet-ink signatures for most transactions. Additional state law: California does NOT have an additional state e-signature law beyond UETA — ESIGN Act applies for interstate commerce.

**Specific rules:**
- Both parties must consent to electronic transactions
- Consumer disclosure requirements mirror federal ESIGN Act
- California courts have upheld click-wrap and browse-wrap agreements, but with heightened scrutiny for browse-wrap
- Notarization: California permits remote online notarization (RON) effective January 1, 2030 (SB 696, signed 2024)

## Non-Compete Agreements
California Business and Professions Code Section 16600 renders non-compete agreements generally VOID and UNENFORCEABLE.

**Exceptions:**
- Sale of a business: Seller can agree not to compete within the geographic area of the sold business
- Dissolution of a partnership: Departing partner can agree not to compete
- Dissolution/sale of LLC membership

**AB 1076 (2020) and SB 699 (2024):** California further strengthened non-compete protections:
- Employers cannot require employees to sign non-competes, even if governed by another state's law
- California residents are protected regardless of where the contract is signed
- Employers must notify current and former employees that void non-compete clauses are unenforceable

**Non-solicitation:** California courts have increasingly struck down customer non-solicitation agreements that effectively function as non-competes.

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 4 years (Code of Civil Procedure 337)
- **Oral contracts:** 2 years (Code of Civil Procedure 339)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC, California Commercial Code 2725)
- **Professional negligence:** 1 year from discovery, max 4 years from act (CCP 340.5 for legal malpractice; CCP 338 for other professionals)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 30 days for tenancy under 1 year; 60 days for tenancy over 1 year (Civil Code 1946.1)
- **Commercial lease default:** Typically per lease terms; no statutory minimum unless specified
- **Contract breach notice:** Per contract terms; no general statutory requirement

## California-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit Limits (Residential)
- **Maximum:** 1 month's rent for unfurnished; 2 months' rent for furnished (effective July 1, 2024, under AB 12, reduced from previous 2/3 month limits)
- **Return deadline:** 21 days after tenant vacates
- **Itemized deductions required** with receipts
- **Cannot charge nonrefundable deposits** (except for screening fee)
- **Cannot charge pet deposit for service/support animals**

### Contractor Licensing
California requires contractor licensing (CSLB) for all projects over $500. Contracts with unlicensed contractors for work requiring a license are VOID — the contractor cannot sue to collect payment (Business and Professions Code 7031).

### Consumer Protection
- **Unfair Competition Law (UCL, Bus. & Prof. Code 17200):** Broad prohibition on unfair, unlawful, or fraudulent business practices
- **CCPA/CPRA:** California Consumer Privacy Act and California Privacy Rights Act impose data handling requirements that affect contract terms for businesses handling California consumer data
- **Auto-renewal (ARL):** Automatic Renewal Law (Bus. & Prof. Code 17600) requires clear disclosure, affirmative consent, and easy cancellation for auto-renewing contracts

### Mechanic's Lien
- **Preliminary notice:** 20 days from first furnishing labor/materials (Civil Code 8200)
- **Lien filing deadline:** 90 days after completion (Civil Code 8412)
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 90 days after recording lien (Civil Code 8460)
- **Stop payment notice** available as alternative to lien for public works
