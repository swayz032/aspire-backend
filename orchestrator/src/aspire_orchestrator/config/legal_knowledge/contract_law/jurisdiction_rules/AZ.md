# Arizona Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Arizona adopted UETA (ARS 44-7001 through 44-7051, effective 2000). Arizona also passed the Arizona Electronic Transactions Act (AETA) as an early adopter.

**Specific rules:**
- Electronic signatures have full legal equivalence
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under ARS 41-371 et seq. (effective 2023)
- Arizona courts have consistently upheld e-signatures in commercial and real estate contexts

## Non-Compete Agreements
Arizona enforces non-competes under common law reasonableness analysis. No specific statute restricting non-competes.

**Enforceability factors:**
- Must protect a legitimate business interest
- Must be reasonable in scope, geographic area, and duration
- Must not impose undue hardship on the employee
- Must be supported by consideration (employment is sufficient for new hires; continued employment may be sufficient for existing employees — courts are inconsistent)

**Arizona-specific:**
- Courts will blue-pencil overly broad covenants (Olliver/Taber/Vitas framework)
- Arizona Supreme Court endorsed the "employee choice" doctrine — if an employee voluntarily leaves and competes, the non-compete is more likely to be enforced than if the employee was terminated
- Non-solicitation agreements generally enforced under same framework
- Typical enforceable duration: 1-2 years
- Physician non-competes: Enforceable but disfavored; must allow patient continuity of care

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 6 years (ARS 12-548)
- **Oral contracts:** 3 years (ARS 12-543)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC)
- **Fraud:** 3 years from discovery (ARS 12-543)
- **Professional malpractice:** 2 years from injury or discovery (ARS 12-542)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 30 days (ARS 33-1375)
- **Week-to-week tenancy:** 10 days (ARS 33-1375)
- **Eviction (non-payment):** 5-day notice (ARS 33-1368)
- **Material lease violation:** 10-day notice with 5-day cure period (ARS 33-1368)
- **Immediate termination:** Allowed for health/safety violations (ARS 33-1368)

## Arizona-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** 1.5 months' rent (ARS 33-1321)
- **Return deadline:** 14 business days after tenant vacates (ARS 33-1321)
- **Itemized deductions required** with written statement
- **Interest:** Not required
- **Penalty:** If landlord wrongfully withholds, tenant may recover 2x the amount wrongfully withheld
- **Nonrefundable fees:** Arizona permits nonrefundable fees (cleaning, pet, redecorating) IF disclosed in writing and designated as nonrefundable (distinct from refundable deposit)

### Contractor Licensing
Arizona requires contractor licensing through the Arizona Registrar of Contractors (ROC):
- **License required** for all projects regardless of value
- **Commercial licenses:** A-General Engineering, B-General Commercial, B-1 through B-5 specialties
- **Residential licenses:** A-General Engineering, B-General Residential, B-1 through B-5 specialties
- **Dual licenses:** Required if doing both commercial and residential
- **Separate licenses:** CR (Commercial Residential), KA-6 (HVAC), K-37 (Plumbing), K-11 (Electrical)
- **Unlicensed work:** Class 1 misdemeanor; cannot recover payment; ROC can order restitution

### Mechanic's Lien (ARS 33-981 et seq.)
- **Preliminary 20-day notice:** Required for all claimants except GC (ARS 33-992.01) — must be served within 20 days of first furnishing
- **Lien filing deadline:** 120 days from completion (ARS 33-993)
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 6 months from recording (ARS 33-998)
- **Residential protection:** Homeowner can request and receive a 10-day preliminary notice from potential lien claimants

### Consumer Fraud Act (ARS 44-1521 et seq.)
- Prohibits deception, deceptive or unfair acts in sale/advertisement of goods/services
- Private right of action for actual damages (may include consequential and punitive)
- Attorney fees recoverable
- Treble damages for "willful" violations (ARS 44-1528)
- Applies to consumer and some commercial transactions

### Arizona Community Property
Arizona is a community property state. Contract implications:
- Contracts creating community debt may bind both spouses' community property
- Sole proprietors should clarify whether contracts are sole/separate or community obligations
- Real property transactions require both spouses' signatures for community property

### Prevailing Party Attorney Fees
Arizona has a unique statute (ARS 12-341.01) that allows courts to award attorney fees to the prevailing party in any contested contract action. This is broader than most states and significantly affects the economics of contract litigation.
