# Texas Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Texas adopted UETA (Texas Business and Commerce Code Chapter 322, effective 2002). Electronic signatures are legally equivalent to wet-ink signatures for most transactions.

**Specific rules:**
- Both parties must agree to conduct business electronically
- Texas courts have consistently upheld e-signatures in commercial contexts
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under Texas Government Code Chapter 406, effective 2018 (Texas was an early adopter)

## Non-Compete Agreements
Texas enforces non-competes under the Texas Business and Commerce Code Section 15.50-15.52 (Covenants Not to Compete Act).

**Enforceability requirements:**
- Must be ancillary to or part of an otherwise enforceable agreement
- Must be reasonable in time, geographic area, and scope of activity
- Must not impose greater restraint than necessary to protect business interests
- Must be supported by consideration (employment + confidential information or specialized training typically suffices)

**Texas-specific factors:**
- At-will employment alone is NOT sufficient consideration — must have additional consideration (e.g., access to confidential information, specialized training, stock options)
- Courts will reform (not void) overly broad covenants to make them reasonable
- Typical enforceable duration: 1-2 years
- Typical enforceable geographic scope: area where employee worked or had client relationships

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 4 years (CPRC 16.004)
- **Oral contracts:** 4 years (CPRC 16.004)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC, Texas Business and Commerce Code 2.725)
- **Fraud:** 4 years (CPRC 16.004(a)(4))
- **Debt on account:** 4 years (CPRC 16.004)
- **Professional malpractice (legal):** 2 years (CPRC 16.003)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 1 month (Texas Property Code 91.001) — can be modified by lease
- **Commercial lease default:** Per lease terms
- **Mechanic's lien notice:** See below

## Texas-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** No statutory limit
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after tenant vacates (Texas Property Code 92.103)
- **Itemized deductions required** with written description
- **Bad faith retention:** Landlord liable for 3x deposit + $100 + attorney fees for bad faith retention
- **No interest requirement** on deposits

### Contractor Licensing
Texas does NOT have a statewide general contractor license. However:
- Electrical contractors: Licensed by TDLR
- Plumbers: Licensed by Texas State Board of Plumbing Examiners
- HVAC: Licensed by TDLR
- Some municipalities require additional local permits and registrations

### Texas-Specific Contract Provisions
- **Texas Deceptive Trade Practices Act (DTPA):** Broad consumer protection statute. Consumers can recover actual damages, treble damages for knowing violations, and attorney fees. Businesses with assets over $25M or 100+ employees may waive DTPA in writing.
- **Anti-indemnity statute (Chapter 151, Insurance Code):** In construction contracts, indemnification for the indemnitee's sole or concurrent negligence is VOID
- **Prompt Payment Act (Government Code Ch. 2251):** Government entities must pay contractors within 30 days or pay interest at rate set by Comptroller
- **Texas Property Code Chapter 53 (Mechanic's Lien):**
  - Preliminary notice: Sub/supplier must send notice to owner by 15th day of 2nd month after labor/materials first furnished
  - Lien filing deadline: 15th day of 4th month after work completed (for projects with retainage) or 15th day of 3rd month (without retainage)
  - Foreclosure deadline: 1 year after filing (extended to 2 years in some cases)
  - Residential projects: Additional protections for homeowners (Chapter 53, Subchapter I)

### Community Property
Texas is a community property state. For contracts involving significant assets:
- Both spouses may need to sign for community property transactions
- Sole proprietor contracts may bind community property
- Clara should flag when personal guarantees may affect community property

### Homestead Protection
Texas has strong homestead protections. Mechanic's liens on homesteads require additional compliance (written contract signed by both spouses before work begins for new construction improvements).
