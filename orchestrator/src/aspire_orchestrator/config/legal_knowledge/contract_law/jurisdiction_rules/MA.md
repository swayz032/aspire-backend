# Massachusetts Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Massachusetts adopted UETA (MGL Chapter 110G, effective 2004). Electronic signatures have full legal equivalence.

**Specific rules:**
- Massachusetts Uniform Electronic Transactions Act covers most commercial and consumer transactions
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under Chapter 222 of the Acts of 2020 (initially COVID emergency, extended and made permanent via subsequent legislation)
- Massachusetts courts have upheld e-signatures in employment agreements, non-competes, and real estate

## Non-Compete Agreements
Massachusetts enacted the Massachusetts Noncompetition Agreement Act (MGL Chapter 149, Section 24L, effective October 1, 2018).

**Key restrictions:**
- Non-competes VOID for: nonexempt employees under FLSA, undergraduate/graduate students (interns/co-ops), employees terminated without cause or laid off, employees under 18
- Maximum duration: 12 months (may extend to 24 months if employee breaches fiduciary duty or steals property)
- Must be supported by: mutually agreed-upon garden leave OR other mutually agreed-upon consideration
- **Garden leave requirement:** Employer must pay at least 50% of base salary during non-compete period, OR provide other mutually agreed-upon consideration
- Must be in writing and signed by both parties
- Must include a right to consult an attorney
- Must be provided at or before the earlier of: formal offer or 10 business days before employment begins; for existing employees, 10 business days before effective date plus fair and reasonable consideration

**Enforceability factors:**
- Must protect legitimate business interests (trade secrets, confidential information, goodwill)
- Must be reasonable in geographic scope (limited to areas where employee worked)
- Must be consonant with public policy
- Courts may reform overly broad covenants

**Non-solicitation and confidentiality agreements:** NOT subject to the Act — may be entered into with broader categories of workers.

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 6 years (MGL Chapter 260, Section 2)
- **Oral contracts:** 6 years (MGL Chapter 260, Section 2)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC)
- **Fraud:** 3 years (MGL Chapter 260, Section 2A) — 6 years for fraud on a creditor
- **Professional malpractice (legal):** 3 years from discovery (MGL Chapter 260, Section 4)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (at-will/month-to-month):** 30 days or 1 rental period (whichever is longer) (MGL Chapter 186, Section 12)
- **Rent increase:** 30 days (no specific statute; per common law and lease terms)
- **Eviction (non-payment):** 14-day notice to quit (MGL Chapter 186, Section 11)
- **Lease violation (other):** 30-day notice to quit

## Massachusetts-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** 1 month's rent (MGL Chapter 186, Section 15B) — one of the strictest in the US
- **Last month's rent:** May be collected separately (also limited to 1 month's rent, must pay interest)
- **Lock change deposit:** Up to cost of key/lock change
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after tenant vacates
- **Interest required:** Must hold in interest-bearing account in Massachusetts bank; pay interest annually to tenant or credit against rent (5% or actual interest, whichever is less)
- **Condition statement required:** Written statement of condition within 10 days of tenant occupancy
- **Penalty for violations:** 3x damages for wrongful withholding, plus attorney fees and court costs; failure to hold in escrow: automatic forfeiture of right to retain any deposit
- **No pet deposit allowed** — included in 1-month maximum

### Contractor Licensing
Massachusetts requires contractor licensing:
- **Home Improvement Contractor (HIC):** Registration required for all residential work (MGL Chapter 142A)
- **Construction Supervisor License:** Required for all residential construction — obtained from BBRS (Board of Building Regulations and Standards)
- **Separate licenses:** Plumbing, gas fitting, electrical, sheet metal
- **Written contract required** for home improvement projects over $1,000
- **Arbitration:** Disputes under $10,000 may be submitted to arbitration under HIC program

### Mechanic's Lien (MGL Chapter 254)
- **Notice of Identification:** Sub/supplier must file with registry of deeds and serve on owner within 60 days of starting work or first delivery
- **Statement of account:** Must be filed within 90 days of last furnishing
- **Dissolution of lien:** Lien dissolved if suit not filed within 90 days of filing statement
- **Bond:** Owner can file bond to discharge lien
- **Residential projects:** Additional requirements for 1-4 family dwellings

### Chapter 93A (Consumer Protection)
Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 93A is one of the most powerful consumer protection statutes in the US:
- Prohibits unfair or deceptive acts in trade/commerce
- Private right of action: actual damages, or up to 3x damages for knowing/willful violations, plus attorney fees
- Demand letter required: Must send 30-day demand letter before filing suit; failure to offer reasonable settlement within 30 days triggers treble damages
- Applies to both consumer transactions and business-to-business transactions (Section 11 for B2B)
- B2B claims require "trade or commerce" nexus — pure private disputes not covered
- Attorney fees are mandatory for prevailing plaintiffs

### Wage Act (MGL Chapter 149, Section 148)
Relevant to independent contractor agreements: Massachusetts has strict worker classification standards. The "ABC test" presumes workers are employees unless ALL three conditions met:
- (A) Free from control and direction in performing services
- (B) Services performed outside usual course of employer's business
- (C) Worker is customarily engaged in independent trade/occupation
Failure to meet all three = employee. Penalties include treble damages.
