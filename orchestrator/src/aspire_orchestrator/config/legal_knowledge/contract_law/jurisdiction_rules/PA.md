# Pennsylvania Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Pennsylvania adopted UETA (73 Pa. Stat. 2260.101-2260.5101, effective 1999 — one of the first states). Electronic signatures have full legal equivalence.

**Specific rules:**
- Pennsylvania was an early UETA adopter
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under Act 97 of 2020 (initially COVID emergency, made permanent)
- Pennsylvania courts have consistently upheld e-signatures in employment and commercial contexts

## Non-Compete Agreements
Pennsylvania enforces non-competes under common law reasonableness analysis. No specific statute governing non-competes.

**Enforceability factors:**
- Must protect a legitimate business interest (trade secrets, confidential information, goodwill, specialized training)
- Must be reasonable in duration (1-2 years typical)
- Must be reasonable in geographic scope
- Must be supported by adequate consideration (new employment is sufficient for new hires; for existing employees, some courts require additional consideration — this is unsettled)
- Must not impose undue hardship

**Pennsylvania-specific:**
- Courts apply a strict approach to reasonableness — will not blue-pencil overly broad covenants (some courts); other courts have reformed. The law is inconsistent across districts.
- Post-termination restrictions generally not enforced if employee was terminated without cause (some courts — not uniformly applied)

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 4 years (42 Pa.C.S. 5525(a)(8))
- **Oral contracts:** 4 years (42 Pa.C.S. 5525(a)(8))
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC)
- **Fraud:** 2 years from discovery (42 Pa.C.S. 5524(7))
- **Professional malpractice:** 2 years (42 Pa.C.S. 5524(2))

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 15 days for leases under 1 year; 30 days for leases of 1+ year (68 Pa.C.S. 250.501)
- **Eviction (non-payment):** 10-day notice (68 Pa.C.S. 250.501)
- **Commercial lease:** Per lease terms

## Pennsylvania-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** 2 months' rent for first year; 1 month's rent for subsequent years (68 Pa.C.S. 250.511a)
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after lease termination
- **Itemized deductions required** with list of damages
- **Interest:** Required after 2 years of tenancy — deposit must be in interest-bearing escrow account; tenant receives interest minus 1% administrative fee
- **Escrow requirement:** Landlord with 25+ units must hold deposit in escrow in PA banking institution

### Contractor Licensing
Pennsylvania does not have a statewide general contractor license. However:
- Home improvement contractors: Must register with the Attorney General's office (Home Improvement Consumer Protection Act, 73 Pa.C.S. 517.1-517.18)
- Registration required for all contractors performing home improvements > $500
- Plumbers and electricians: Licensed at municipal/county level
- Philadelphia requires specific contractor licenses

### Mechanic's Lien (49 P.S. 1101-1902)
- **Preliminary notice:** Not required for GC; sub/supplier must file with prothonotary
- **Lien filing deadline:** 6 months from completion
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 2 years from filing
- **Residential protection:** For residential properties, lien only applies if there is a written contract between homeowner and GC

### Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law (UTPCPL)
- 73 Pa.C.S. 201-1 et seq.
- Private right of action for treble damages
- Broad definition of deceptive conduct
- Attorney fees recoverable by prevailing consumer

### Anti-Indemnity Statute
Pennsylvania's anti-indemnity statute (68 Pa.C.S. 491) applies to construction contracts and prohibits agreements requiring a party to indemnify another for its own negligence.
