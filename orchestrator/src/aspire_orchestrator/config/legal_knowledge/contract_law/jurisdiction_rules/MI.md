# Michigan Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Michigan adopted UETA (MCL 450.831 et seq., effective 2004). Electronic signatures have full legal equivalence.

**Specific rules:**
- Michigan Uniform Electronic Transactions Act applies to all electronic transactions where parties consent
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under Executive Order 2020-74 (COVID emergency), formalized by legislation
- Michigan courts have upheld e-signatures in real estate and commercial contexts

## Non-Compete Agreements
Michigan enforces non-competes under the Michigan Antitrust Reform Act (MCL 445.774a, effective 1987).

**Key statutory provisions:**
- Non-competes are enforceable IF reasonable in duration, geographic area, and type of employment/line of business
- Must protect reasonable competitive business interests (trade secrets, confidential information, investment in training)
- Courts may reform (modify) unreasonable covenants to make them reasonable — Michigan expressly permits blue-penciling
- No specific salary thresholds or minimum consideration requirements beyond normal contract consideration

**Michigan-specific:**
- Pre-1987 contracts governed by the old Michigan Statute (MCL 445.761 et seq.) which was MORE restrictive
- Non-solicitation agreements also governed by 445.774a and subject to same reasonableness analysis
- Forfeiture-for-competition clauses (e.g., forfeiting unvested stock) may be analyzed differently from traditional non-competes

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 6 years (MCL 600.5807(8))
- **Oral contracts:** 6 years (MCL 600.5807(8))
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC)
- **Fraud:** 6 years from commission or 2 years from discovery, whichever is later (MCL 600.5813, 600.5855)
- **Professional malpractice:** 2 years from discovery, max 6 years from act (MCL 600.5838a)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 1 month (MCL 554.134)
- **Eviction (non-payment):** 7-day demand for possession (MCL 600.5714(1)(c))
- **Lease violation (other):** 30-day notice (MCL 600.5714(1)(d))

## Michigan-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** 1.5 months' rent (MCL 554.602)
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after tenant vacates (MCL 554.609)
- **Move-in checklist required:** Within 7 days of move-in (MCL 554.608)
- **Itemized deductions required**
- **Separate bank account required** — cannot commingle with landlord's personal funds (MCL 554.604)
- **Interest:** Not required
- **Penalty:** Failure to comply: tenant may recover 2x deposit amount

### Contractor Licensing
Michigan requires residential contractor licensing through the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA):
- **Residential Builder license:** Required for all residential construction, remodeling, or alteration
- **Maintenance and Alteration Contractor:** For projects under $600 that do not require structural changes
- **Separate licenses:** Electrical, plumbing, mechanical (HVAC)
- **Unlicensed work:** Misdemeanor; contractor cannot sue for payment; homeowner may recover amounts paid

### Mechanic's Lien (Construction Lien Act, MCL 570.1101 et seq.)
- **Notice of furnishing:** Sub/supplier must provide within 20 days of first furnishing (MCL 570.1109)
- **Sworn statement:** Required before payment (MCL 570.1110)
- **Lien filing deadline:** 90 days from last furnishing (MCL 570.1111)
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 1 year from last furnishing (MCL 570.1117)
- **Residential protection:** Lien on residential property limited to unpaid contract balance between owner and GC

### Michigan Consumer Protection Act (MCL 445.901 et seq.)
- Prohibits unfair, unconscionable, or deceptive methods in trade/commerce
- Private right of action for actual damages or $250 minimum
- Attorney fees and costs recoverable
- Class actions permitted
- Exemption: transactions where a regulatory body has oversight (some courts interpret broadly)

### Right to Cure (Residential Construction)
Michigan has a Right to Repair Act (MCL 600.3001-3020) requiring homeowners to give residential builders/contractors written notice and opportunity to inspect and repair before filing a construction defect lawsuit. 90-day notice required.
