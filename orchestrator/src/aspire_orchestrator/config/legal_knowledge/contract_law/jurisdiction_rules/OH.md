# Ohio Contract Law — Key Variations

## E-Signature Rules
Ohio adopted UETA (Ohio Revised Code Chapter 1306, effective 2000). Electronic signatures are legally equivalent to wet-ink signatures.

**Specific rules:**
- Ohio Uniform Electronic Transactions Act applies to all electronic transactions where parties agree to conduct business electronically
- Remote online notarization (RON) permitted under HB 197 (effective 2020, initially COVID emergency, made permanent via SB 264)

## Non-Compete Agreements
Ohio enforces non-competes under common law reasonableness analysis. No specific statute.

**Enforceability factors:**
- Must be no greater than required to protect employer's legitimate business interest
- Must not impose undue hardship on the employee
- Must not be injurious to the public
- Must be reasonable in time and geographic scope
- Consideration: New employment is sufficient for new hires; continued employment may be sufficient for existing employees if covenant is entered into during employment

**Ohio-specific:**
- Courts will blue-pencil overly broad covenants (modify rather than void)
- Garden leave provisions (paying employee during non-compete period) increase enforceability
- Physician non-competes are enforceable but subject to heightened scrutiny

## Statute of Limitations for Contract Disputes
- **Written contracts:** 8 years (ORC 2305.06) — extended from 6 to 8 in 2012 revision
- **Oral contracts:** 6 years (ORC 2305.07)
- **Breach of warranty (goods):** 4 years (UCC, ORC 1302.98)
- **Fraud:** 4 years from discovery (ORC 2305.09)
- **Professional malpractice (legal):** 1 year from discovery, max 4 years from act (ORC 2305.11)

## Notice Requirements
- **Residential lease termination (month-to-month):** 30 days (ORC 5321.17)
- **Eviction (non-payment):** 3-day notice (ORC 1923.04)
- **Lease violation (other):** 30-day notice with reasonable time to cure (ORC 5321.11)

## Ohio-Specific Contract Rules

### Security Deposit (Residential)
- **Maximum:** No statutory limit
- **Return deadline:** 30 days after tenant vacates (ORC 5321.16)
- **Itemized deductions required**
- **Interest:** Not required
- **Penalty:** If landlord fails to return within 30 days and fails to itemize, tenant may recover deposit amount plus damages in an amount equal to the amount wrongfully withheld, plus attorney fees

### Contractor Licensing
Ohio does not have a statewide general contractor license for residential construction. However:
- Electrical contractors: Licensed by Ohio DICO
- Plumbers: Licensed by Ohio DICO
- HVAC: Licensed by Ohio DICO
- Commercial construction: May require registration depending on municipality
- Many municipalities (Columbus, Cleveland, Cincinnati) have local contractor registration requirements

### Mechanic's Lien (ORC Chapter 1311)
- **Preliminary notice (affidavit):** Sub/supplier must file within 21 days of first furnishing (ORC 1311.05)
- **Lien filing deadline:** 60 days from completion or last furnishing (ORC 1311.06)
- **Foreclosure deadline:** 6 years from filing
- **Notice to commence:** Sub can serve Notice to Commence to trigger owner's 75-day duty to complete or face lien

### Consumer Sales Practices Act (ORC 1345)
- Prohibits unfair, deceptive, or unconscionable consumer sales practices
- Private right of action for actual damages or $200 minimum, plus attorney fees
- Treble damages for knowing violations
- Class action permitted

### Construction Contract Anti-Indemnity
Ohio has no specific anti-indemnity statute for construction contracts, but courts may refuse to enforce indemnification for the indemnitee's own sole negligence under general contract principles.
