# Payment Terms Reference

## Standard Payment Terms

### Net Terms
- **Net 30:** Payment due 30 days from invoice date. Most common for B2B transactions.
- **Net 15:** Payment due 15 days from invoice date. Common for small service providers.
- **Net 60:** Payment due 60 days from invoice date. Common for larger clients or government contracts.
- **Net 90:** Payment due 90 days from invoice date. Typically only for large enterprises or government.
- **Due on receipt:** Payment due immediately upon invoice delivery.

### Early Payment Discounts
- **2/10 Net 30:** 2% discount if paid within 10 days; full amount due in 30 days. Standard trade discount.
- **1/10 Net 30:** 1% discount if paid within 10 days.
- **Annualized return:** 2/10 Net 30 equates to ~36% annualized return — it almost always makes financial sense for the payer to take the discount.

### Selecting Payment Terms
**Factors for choosing terms:**
- **Cash flow needs:** Shorter terms improve cash flow for the service provider
- **Client relationship:** Established clients may expect Net 30+; new clients should start at shorter terms
- **Industry norms:** Construction often uses progress billing; accounting uses monthly retainer; SaaS uses annual prepaid
- **Contract value:** Higher-value contracts may warrant longer terms but with progress payments

## Milestone-Based Payment

### Structure
Payment tied to completion of specific project milestones or deliverables.

**Common structure:**
- 25% upon contract signing (mobilization/deposit)
- 25% upon completion of Phase 1
- 25% upon completion of Phase 2
- 25% upon final completion and acceptance

### When to Use
- Construction projects (most common)
- Software development projects
- Consulting engagements with defined phases
- Creative projects (design, marketing campaigns)

### Key Provisions
- Clear definition of what constitutes "completion" of each milestone
- Acceptance criteria (objective, measurable)
- Timeline for acceptance review (e.g., "Client has 5 business days to review and approve")
- Consequences of delay in acceptance (deemed accepted after review period)
- Retainage provisions (withholding a percentage until final completion)

## Retainer Models

### Earned Retainer (Prepaid Services)
Client pays in advance for a block of hours or services. As services are performed, the retainer is drawn down.

**Key provisions:**
- Hourly rate applied against retainer balance
- Monthly statement of hours/services consumed
- Refund policy for unused retainer balance
- Replenishment requirement when balance drops below threshold

### Unearned Retainer (Trust Account)
Advance payment held in trust until services are performed. Common for attorneys and some CPAs.

**Key provisions:**
- Funds held in segregated trust/escrow account
- Drawn down as services are performed with monthly accounting
- Unexpended balance refundable upon termination
- Cannot commingle with practitioner's operating funds

### Flat Monthly Retainer
Fixed monthly fee for ongoing services regardless of hours worked.

**Key provisions:**
- Scope of services included in the retainer
- Definition of "out-of-scope" work and its billing rate
- Annual review/adjustment mechanism
- Termination notice period (typically 30-60 days)

### Evergreen Retainer
Automatically replenishing retainer. When balance drops below a threshold, client is invoiced to restore the full amount.

**Key provisions:**
- Initial retainer amount
- Replenishment threshold
- Replenishment invoice terms (e.g., due upon receipt)
- Quarterly accounting of retainer usage

## Progress Billing

### Structure
Periodic invoicing (typically monthly) based on percentage of work completed.

**Common in:**
- Construction (most common — "pencil draw" or progress payment application)
- Long-term consulting engagements
- Engineering and architectural services

### AIA Payment Application (Construction)
The American Institute of Architects (AIA) publishes standard forms for construction payment:
- **G702 (Application and Certificate for Payment):** Summary of contract amount, work completed, and amount due
- **G703 (Continuation Sheet):** Line-by-line breakdown of work items, percentage complete, and amounts

### Key Provisions
- Billing frequency (monthly, bi-weekly)
- Method of measuring progress (percentage complete, units installed, cost-based)
- Retainage percentage (5-10% withheld until completion)
- Lien waiver exchange (conditional waiver with each payment application)
- Pay application review period (typically 7-10 days for review, 30 days for payment)

## Late Payment Provisions

### Late Payment Penalties
- **Flat late fee:** Fixed dollar amount (e.g., $25-$50 per month). Simple but may be disproportionate for small invoices.
- **Percentage late fee:** Percentage of outstanding balance per month (e.g., 1.5% per month = 18% APR). Must comply with state usury laws.
- **Interest rate:** Stated annual interest rate on overdue balances. Often "the lesser of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law."

### Usury Limits (State-Specific)
Maximum interest rates on contract debts vary by state:
- **California:** 10% per year for non-exempt transactions (Cal. Const. Art. XV)
- **New York:** 16% per year (NY Gen. Oblig. Law 5-501)
- **Texas:** 18% per year for written agreements; 6% for oral (Tex. Fin. Code 302.001)
- **Florida:** 18% per year (Fla. Stat. 687.02); 25% constitutes criminal usury
- **Many states:** No usury limit for commercial/business transactions above certain thresholds

### Acceleration Clause
Allows the entire remaining balance to become due immediately upon default:
"If any payment is not received within [X] days of its due date, the entire unpaid balance shall become immediately due and payable."

### Collection Costs
"In the event of collection action, the defaulting party shall be responsible for all costs of collection, including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs."

### Suspension of Services
"If payment is not received within [X] days of the due date, Provider may suspend services upon [Y] days' written notice. Suspension of services shall not relieve Client of payment obligations for services already rendered."

## Deposit and Advance Payment

### Deposits for Services
- Typical range: 25-50% of total project cost
- **Refundable vs. nonrefundable:** Specify clearly in contract
- **Application:** "Deposit shall be applied against the final invoice" vs. "Deposit is nonrefundable and represents commitment to project scheduling"
- **Earned upon receipt vs. held in trust:** Important distinction for accounting and legal purposes

### Construction Deposits
- Home improvement statutes in some states limit deposits (e.g., California: cannot exceed $1,000 or 10% of contract price, whichever is less, for contracts over $7,500)
- Deposits for materials may be higher if materials are specified and ordered

### Retainer vs. Deposit
- **Retainer:** Ongoing payment for availability or services over time
- **Deposit:** One-time payment securing a specific project or service
- The distinction matters for accounting (revenue recognition), legal (refund obligations), and tax purposes

## Tax Implications in Payment Terms

### Sales Tax on Services
Whether services are subject to sales tax varies by state and service type:
- Most states do NOT tax professional services (legal, accounting, consulting)
- Some states tax specific services (TX taxes some services; HI taxes all services; NM taxes all services)
- Construction labor is generally NOT taxed; materials ARE taxed
- SaaS is increasingly taxed as states expand digital goods taxation

### 1099 Reporting
Payments to independent contractors $600+ in a calendar year must be reported on Form 1099-NEC:
- Payments to corporations (except medical/legal) are exempt from 1099 reporting
- Payments to LLCs taxed as partnerships or sole proprietors require 1099 reporting
- Collect W-9 before making first payment

### Backup Withholding
If a contractor does not provide a valid TIN (via W-9), the payer may be required to withhold 24% of payments for federal income tax (backup withholding).
