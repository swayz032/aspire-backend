# Compliance Patterns

## Data Privacy in Contracts

### CCPA / CPRA (California)
The California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act) affects contracts where a business handles California consumer personal information.

**Contract requirements when engaging service providers:**
- Written agreement specifying the business purpose for data processing
- Prohibition on selling or sharing consumer personal information received from the business
- Prohibition on processing data for purposes other than those specified in the contract
- Obligation to implement and maintain reasonable security measures
- Right for the business to audit compliance
- Obligation to notify the business of subprocessor engagements
- Obligation to assist with consumer rights requests (access, deletion, correction)

**When Clara should flag CCPA compliance:**
- Service agreements where the vendor will access customer data
- Bookkeeping/accounting engagements where client customer data is handled
- Any contract involving a California-based consumer-facing business

### GDPR Basics (If Serving EU Clients)
If the Aspire user serves any EU/EEA clients or customers, GDPR may apply:

**Contract requirements (Data Processing Agreement):**
- Lawful basis for processing must be identified
- Processor must only process data per controller's instructions
- Data security measures must be specified
- Sub-processor approval required
- Data breach notification within 72 hours
- Data return/deletion upon termination
- Right to audit

**When Clara should flag GDPR:**
- Any contract where EU/EEA personal data is involved
- Vendor agreements where vendor is based in EU/EEA
- Professional service agreements serving EU/EEA clients

### State Privacy Laws (Expanding)
Beyond California, several states have enacted comprehensive privacy laws:
- **Virginia (VCDPA):** Effective Jan 2023
- **Colorado (CPA):** Effective Jul 2023
- **Connecticut (CTDPA):** Effective Jul 2023
- **Utah (UCPA):** Effective Dec 2023
- **Texas (TDPSA):** Effective Jul 2024
- **Oregon (OCPA):** Effective Jul 2024
- **Montana (MCDPA):** Effective Oct 2024

Common contractual requirements across state privacy laws:
- Data processing agreements with service providers
- Consumer rights compliance mechanisms
- Data security obligations
- Breach notification procedures

## Employment Law in Contracts

### Worker Classification Compliance
Misclassifying employees as independent contractors creates significant legal and financial risk.

**IRS 20-Factor Test (Common Law):** Examines behavioral control, financial control, and type of relationship.

**ABC Test (Used by CA, MA, NJ, IL, and others):**
- (A) Worker is free from control and direction
- (B) Work is outside the usual course of the hiring entity's business
- (C) Worker is customarily engaged in an independently established trade or occupation

**Contract provisions that support IC classification:**
- Contractor controls method and manner of work
- Contractor uses own tools and equipment
- Contractor can hire helpers/subcontractors
- Contractor works for multiple clients
- Contractor invoices for payment (no withholding)
- Defined project scope with deliverables (not ongoing duties)
- No benefits provided
- Either party can terminate with notice

**Contract provisions that risk misclassification:**
- Requiring specific work hours or location
- Providing tools, equipment, or workspace
- Prohibiting work for other clients
- Providing training on how to perform the work
- Paying a regular salary or hourly wage on payroll schedule
- Integrating worker into organizational structure (company email, title, org chart)

### Equal Employment Opportunity
Contracts related to hiring (including independent contractor agreements) must not contain discriminatory provisions:
- Cannot restrict based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin (Title VII)
- Cannot restrict based on age 40+ (ADEA)
- Cannot restrict based on disability (ADA)
- Cannot restrict based on genetic information (GINA)
- State/local laws may add additional protected classes (sexual orientation, gender identity, marital status, etc.)

### Non-Discrimination in Subcontracting
Government contracts and many private contracts include non-discrimination clauses:
- Must provide equal opportunity in subcontracting
- Must not discriminate in selection of subcontractors
- May require affirmative action in subcontractor selection (government contracts)

## Tax Obligations in Contracts

### Sales Tax Collection
Contracts for goods and certain services may trigger sales tax obligations:
- Seller/provider must collect and remit sales tax
- Contract should specify whether stated prices include or exclude sales tax
- "Plus applicable taxes" is the standard commercial approach
- Nexus rules determine which states require tax collection (physical presence, economic nexus, marketplace facilitator rules)

### Use Tax Obligations
If a business purchases goods from out-of-state vendors who don't collect sales tax, the business may owe use tax in their home state. Contracts should not encourage use tax evasion.

### Withholding Tax on Services
Certain states require withholding on payments to out-of-state contractors performing services within the state:
- Construction projects (many states require withholding from non-resident contractors)
- Performance services
- Amount varies by state (typically the state income tax rate)

### Property Tax Implications
Leases and equipment agreements may trigger personal property tax obligations:
- Equipment leases: Who is responsible for property tax on leased equipment?
- Real estate leases: Triple-net leases pass property tax to tenant
- Software licenses: Some states tax software as tangible personal property

## Insurance Requirements in Contracts

### Standard Insurance Requirements
Most B2B contracts require one or both parties to maintain:

**Commercial General Liability (CGL):**
- Typical minimum: $1M per occurrence, $2M aggregate
- Covers bodily injury, property damage, personal/advertising injury
- Additional insured endorsement often required

**Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions):**
- Typical minimum: $1M per claim, $2M aggregate
- Required for professional services (consulting, accounting, legal, technology)
- Covers negligent acts, errors, and omissions in professional services

**Workers' Compensation:**
- Statutory limits (required by law in most states)
- Must be maintained if contractor has employees
- Employer's liability: typically $500K/$500K/$500K
- Some states allow sole proprietors/single-member LLCs to opt out

**Commercial Automobile:**
- Required if contractor uses vehicles for work
- Typical minimum: $1M combined single limit

**Umbrella/Excess Liability:**
- Provides additional coverage above primary policy limits
- Typical minimum: $1M-$5M depending on contract value/risk

### Certificate of Insurance (COI)
- Standard proof of insurance coverage
- ACORD form is the industry standard
- Must list the counterparty as "Certificate Holder"
- Additional insured status requires endorsement (not just listing on COI)
- Verify coverage dates span the entire contract term
- Request 30-day advance notice of cancellation/material change

### Waiver of Subrogation
Common in construction contracts: Insurer waives right to pursue claims against the other party's insurer. Must be added as endorsement to the insurance policy. Both parties typically carry mutual waiver of subrogation.

## Regulatory Compliance Clauses

### Anti-Corruption
For contracts involving government work or international business:
- Compliance with Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA)
- Compliance with state/local ethics laws
- No gifts, payments, or inducements to government officials
- Right to audit compliance

### Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
For financial service contracts:
- Know Your Customer (KYC) obligations
- Transaction monitoring requirements
- Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) filing obligations
- Record retention requirements (5 years minimum under BSA)

### Environmental Compliance
For construction and property contracts:
- Hazardous materials handling and disposal
- Environmental site assessments (Phase I/II)
- Asbestos/lead paint disclosure and handling
- Stormwater management (NPDES permits)
- Wetlands protection
