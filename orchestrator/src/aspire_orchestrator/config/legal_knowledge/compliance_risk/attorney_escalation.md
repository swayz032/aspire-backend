# Attorney Escalation Guide

## When Clara Should Recommend Attorney Review

Clara is a contract management assistant, not a substitute for legal counsel. The following scenarios REQUIRE Clara to recommend that the user consult with a licensed attorney before proceeding. Clara should never attempt to finalize these contracts without attorney involvement.

## Category 1: High-Value Transactions

### Real Estate Transactions Over $500,000
Commercial property purchases, sales, and long-term leases involving significant financial commitment require attorney review for:
- Title examination and title insurance
- Environmental liability assessment (Phase I/II reports)
- Zoning and land use compliance
- Financing document review
- Tax structure optimization (1031 exchanges, opportunity zones)

### Mergers, Acquisitions, and Business Sales
Any transaction involving the sale, merger, or acquisition of a business:
- Asset purchase vs. stock purchase structure
- Due diligence coordination
- Representations and warranties negotiation
- Escrow and indemnification holdbacks
- Non-compete provisions tied to business sale
- Employment transition and benefits continuation
- Intellectual property assignments
- Tax allocation (IRC Section 338(h)(10), 1060)

### Equity and Ownership Changes
Adding or removing partners/members, issuing equity:
- Operating agreement amendments
- Valuation of business interests
- Tax implications of ownership changes
- Drag-along/tag-along rights
- Preferred return structures
- Vesting schedules

## Category 2: Employment Disputes and Settlements

### Separation Agreements
When terminating an employee, especially in contested situations:
- Release of claims requirements
- ADEA/OWBPA compliance (21-day consideration period for employees 40+, 7-day revocation period)
- Confidentiality and non-disparagement
- Continuation of benefits
- Non-compete enforcement post-termination
- COBRA notification obligations

### Non-Compete Enforcement
When considering enforcing or challenging a non-compete:
- State-specific enforceability analysis
- Injunctive relief strategy
- Liquidated damages vs. actual damages
- Trade secret identification and protection
- Blue-pencil analysis

### Employment Discrimination Claims
Any matter involving potential discrimination:
- EEOC charge response
- Internal investigation procedures
- Mediation and conciliation
- Settlement structure and confidentiality

## Category 3: Regulatory and Government Matters

### Government Contracts
Contracts with federal, state, or local government agencies:
- FAR/DFAR compliance (federal)
- Small Business Administration requirements
- Davis-Bacon prevailing wage (construction)
- Buy American/Trade Agreements Act
- Security clearance requirements
- Conflict of interest certifications
- Prompt payment requirements
- Dispute resolution under Contract Disputes Act

### Regulatory Filings
Contracts that require or trigger regulatory filings:
- Securities filings (SEC, state securities regulators)
- Environmental permits (EPA, state DEQ)
- Healthcare certifications (CMS, state health departments)
- Financial services licensing (state banking regulators, FinCEN)

### Tax-Sensitive Structures
Contracts with significant tax implications:
- IRC Section 1031 like-kind exchanges
- Installment sales (IRC Section 453)
- Partnership tax allocations
- Employee vs. independent contractor classification disputes with state agencies
- Qualified Opportunity Zone investments
- Cost segregation studies (for real estate)

## Category 4: Cross-Border and International

### International Contracts
Any agreement involving parties or performance in multiple countries:
- Choice of law and forum selection
- International arbitration (ICC, UNCITRAL)
- Import/export compliance (EAR, ITAR)
- Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA)
- Currency and exchange rate provisions
- International intellectual property protection
- Tax treaty implications

### Multi-State Complex Transactions
Transactions that involve regulatory compliance in multiple states simultaneously:
- Multi-state construction projects
- Interstate franchise agreements
- Nationwide employment agreements
- Multi-state real estate portfolios

## Category 5: High-Risk Legal Structures

### Personal Liability Situations
When the business owner's personal assets may be at risk:
- Personal guarantees for amounts exceeding $50,000
- Piercing the corporate veil concerns
- Community property state implications
- Homestead exemption planning

### Settlement Agreements
Resolution of any legal dispute through settlement:
- Release language and scope
- Confidentiality provisions
- Tax treatment of settlement proceeds
- Structured settlement terms
- Non-admission clauses
- Enforcement mechanisms

### Bankruptcy-Adjacent Situations
When a counterparty is financially distressed:
- Preference avoidance risks (payments within 90 days of bankruptcy filing may be clawed back)
- Executory contract assumptions and rejections
- Adequate assurance of performance
- Priority of claims

## Clara's Escalation Script

When Clara identifies an attorney escalation scenario, she should communicate clearly:

**Standard escalation message:**
"Based on my analysis, this contract involves [specific issue: e.g., significant financial exposure, regulatory complexity, employment law concerns]. I recommend consulting with a licensed attorney who specializes in [relevant practice area] before finalizing this agreement. I can help prepare the contract materials and organize the key issues for your attorney's review."

**What Clara CAN do to help:**
1. Organize the key issues and questions for the attorney
2. Prepare a summary of the business terms agreed upon
3. Generate a draft contract using the appropriate template (labeled as "DRAFT — FOR ATTORNEY REVIEW")
4. Identify specific clauses that need attorney attention
5. Provide relevant jurisdiction-specific information from the knowledge base
6. Calendar the attorney consultation and follow-up

**What Clara should NOT do:**
1. Provide specific legal advice ("You should accept this term because...")
2. Opine on the enforceability of specific provisions
3. Advise on litigation strategy
4. Interpret specific legal precedents or case law
5. Finalize contracts that fall into escalation categories without attorney involvement
6. Discourage the user from seeking legal counsel

## Finding the Right Attorney

Clara can suggest the type of attorney needed based on the contract type:

| Contract Type | Attorney Specialization |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Real estate | Real estate attorney / commercial real estate |
| Business sale/purchase | M&A attorney / business transaction attorney |
| Employment matters | Employment/labor attorney |
| Construction disputes | Construction law attorney |
| Government contracts | Government contracts attorney |
| International | International business attorney |
| Tax-sensitive | Tax attorney or CPA with JD |
| IP-heavy | Intellectual property attorney |
| Franchise | Franchise attorney |
| Securities | Securities attorney |

Clara should recommend attorneys licensed in the relevant jurisdiction. State bar association lawyer referral services are a reliable starting point for small business owners who don't have an existing attorney relationship.
