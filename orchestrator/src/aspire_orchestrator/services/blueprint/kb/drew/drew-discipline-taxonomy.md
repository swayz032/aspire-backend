# Drew Discipline Taxonomy

Drew uses this reference to classify each blueprint sheet into its construction discipline.
A sheet's discipline is determined from its title-block sheet number prefix, drawing title,
and the dominant visual content (plan types, symbols, schedules, notes).

---

## A — Architectural

Architectural sheets document the designed form of the building: spatial layout, enclosures,
finishes, and aesthetic intent. They are the most read-across discipline and typically set
the sheet-count baseline for a project.

**Typical content:** Floor plans, reflected ceiling plans, building elevations, wall sections,
enlarged plan details, room finish schedules, door schedules, window schedules, wall type
legends, general notes.

**Title-block keywords:** `A-`, `ARCH`, `ARCHITECTURAL`, `FLOOR PLAN`, `ELEVATION`, `SECTION`,
`DETAIL`, `RCP` (reflected ceiling plan), `ROOM FINISH SCHEDULE`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** A0.x (cover/general), A1.x (floor plans), A2.x (elevations),
A3.x (sections), A4.x (details), A5.x (schedules).

---

## S — Structural

Structural sheets document the load-bearing framework: how gravity loads, wind loads, and
seismic forces are carried from the roof to the foundations.

**Typical content:** Foundation plans, framing plans (floor and roof), structural sections,
connection details, column schedules, beam schedules, footing schedules, general structural
notes, geotechnical references, lateral-force-resisting system diagrams.

**Title-block keywords:** `S-`, `STRUCT`, `STRUCTURAL`, `FRAMING PLAN`, `FOUNDATION PLAN`,
`SHEAR WALL`, `COLUMN SCHEDULE`, `BEAM SCHEDULE`, `FOOTING`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** S0.x (general/notes), S1.x (foundations), S2.x (framing), S3.x (details).

---

## M — Mechanical

Mechanical sheets document the HVAC (heating, ventilation, air-conditioning) systems that
control temperature, humidity, and air quality throughout the building.

**Typical content:** HVAC floor plans with duct routing, equipment schedules, air-handling unit
details, exhaust fan schedules, diffuser/grille schedules, mechanical room details, controls
diagrams (sequence of operations), energy compliance notes.

**Title-block keywords:** `M-`, `MECH`, `MECHANICAL`, `HVAC`, `DUCTWORK`, `AIR HANDLING`,
`AHU`, `VAV`, `SUPPLY/RETURN AIR`, `EQUIPMENT SCHEDULE`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** M0.x (general), M1.x (HVAC plans), M2.x (schedules/details).

---

## E — Electrical

Electrical sheets document power distribution, lighting, and low-voltage systems.

**Typical content:** Electrical site plans, floor plans showing panel locations, lighting plans,
power plans, one-line diagrams, panel schedules, feeder schedules, load calculations, lighting
fixture schedules, fire alarm riser diagrams, grounding details.

**Title-block keywords:** `E-`, `ELEC`, `ELECTRICAL`, `POWER PLAN`, `LIGHTING PLAN`,
`PANEL SCHEDULE`, `ONE-LINE`, `SERVICE ENTRANCE`, `CIRCUIT`, `CONDUIT`, `FIXTURE SCHEDULE`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** E0.x (general), E1.x (site/power), E2.x (lighting), E3.x (diagrams), E4.x (schedules).

---

## P — Plumbing

Plumbing sheets document the supply, drain, waste, and vent (DWV) piping systems.

**Typical content:** Plumbing floor plans (supply and DWV), isometric riser diagrams, fixture
schedules, roof drainage plans, water heater details, plumbing notes, grease-interceptor details.

**Title-block keywords:** `P-`, `PLUMB`, `PLUMBING`, `DWV`, `DOMESTIC WATER`, `SANITARY`,
`ISOMETRIC`, `RISER DIAGRAM`, `FIXTURE SCHEDULE`, `WATER HEATER`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** P0.x (general), P1.x (floor plans), P2.x (isometrics/details).

---

## FP — Fire Protection

Fire protection sheets document the suppression and detection systems that meet code-required
life-safety standards.

**Typical content:** Sprinkler system plans, sprinkler head schedules, hydraulic calculation
summary sheets, riser diagrams, fire alarm device plans, fire alarm riser/annunciator diagrams,
emergency lighting plans.

**Title-block keywords:** `FP-`, `FA-`, `FIRE`, `FIRE PROTECTION`, `SPRINKLER`, `NFPA`,
`RISER DIAGRAM`, `HYDRAULIC CALC`, `FIRE ALARM`, `ANNUNCIATOR`, `DETECTOR SCHEDULE`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** FP1.x or FA1.x (plans), FP2.x (riser/details).

---

## C — Civil

Civil sheets document site conditions, site work, grading, and utility connections from the
building to the public right-of-way.

**Typical content:** Site plans, grading and drainage plans, utility plans (sewer, water, storm),
demolition plans, erosion/sediment control plans, paving plans, site details, SWPPP notes,
ADA accessible route diagrams.

**Title-block keywords:** `C-`, `CIVIL`, `SITE PLAN`, `GRADING`, `DRAINAGE`, `UTILITY`,
`STORMWATER`, `EROSION`, `PAVING`, `TOPOGRAPHY`, `BENCHMARK`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** C0.x (general/survey), C1.x (site), C2.x (grading), C3.x (utility), C4.x (details).

---

## L — Landscape

Landscape sheets document exterior planting, hardscape, and irrigation systems.

**Typical content:** Planting plans (trees, shrubs, groundcover), planting schedules, irrigation
plans, irrigation controller schedules, hardscape plans, landscape details, maintenance notes.

**Title-block keywords:** `L-`, `LAND`, `LANDSCAPE`, `PLANTING PLAN`, `IRRIGATION`,
`PLANT SCHEDULE`, `HARDSCAPE`, `TREE PROTECTION`.

**Sheet-number conventions:** L1.x (planting), L2.x (irrigation), L3.x (details).

---

## Specs — Project Specifications

The specifications book defines the quality, materials, installation methods, and performance
standards for all construction work. It supplements the drawings with written requirements.

**Typical content:** Table of contents (CSI MasterFormat divisions), Division 01 General
Requirements, product specifications by trade (concrete, masonry, wood framing, roofing,
doors/windows, finishes, HVAC equipment, plumbing fixtures, electrical panels, etc.),
warranty and submittal requirements.

**Title-block keywords:** `SPECS`, `SPECIFICATION`, `PROJECT MANUAL`, `DIVISION`, `CSI`,
`MASTERFORMAT`, `SECTION`, `SUBMITTALS`, `QUALITY ASSURANCE`.

---

## Schedules — Door/Window/Finish Schedules

Stand-alone schedule sheets that tabulate properties of repeating elements, sometimes issued
as separate documents from the architectural set.

**Typical content:** Door schedule (number, size, material, hardware, fire rating), window
schedule (number, size, glazing type, U-value), finish schedule (room-by-room floor/wall/ceiling
finishes), hardware sets, room data sheets.

**Title-block keywords:** `SCHEDULE`, `DOOR SCHEDULE`, `WINDOW SCHEDULE`, `FINISH SCHEDULE`,
`HARDWARE SET`, `ROOM DATA SHEET`.

---

## Addenda — Revisions and Addenda

Addenda sheets supersede earlier drawings or specifications after the original issue date.
They carry a revision number or addendum number and reference the sheets they modify.

**Typical content:** Cloud-marked drawing revisions, replacement sheets, revision clouds,
delta triangle revision tags, addendum cover sheets, change order sketches (SK sheets).

**Title-block keywords:** `ADDENDUM`, `ADDENDA`, `REVISION`, `REV`, `SK-`, `CHANGE ORDER`,
`BULLETIN`, `ASI` (Architect Supplemental Instructions), `RFI`.

**Detection note:** Sheets with a delta-revision indicator (triangle with revision number) in
the title block or a revision table with non-zero entries should be examined for the
addendum/revision classification alongside their primary discipline.
